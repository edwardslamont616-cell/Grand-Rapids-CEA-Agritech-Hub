# Higher Education Integration Model
## GVSU / MSU Capstone + Sponsored Research
**Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub — CEA Campus Design Council**
**Prepared by:** Prof. Elena Cruz, Curriculum Developer
**Frameworks:** Five S + Co-Star + TRACE + CARE
**Date:** 2026

---

## 1. Executive Summary

The Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub's higher education integration strategy rests on two mutually reinforcing mechanisms: (1) a **capstone project hosting program** engaging GVSU undergraduates from CLAS and the Padnos College of Engineering, and (2) a **sponsored research pilot program** connecting MSU AgBioResearch and GVSU faculty with Hub infrastructure to conduct $40,000–$80,000 research pilots that can catalyze larger USDA NIFA AFRI and SCRI competitive grants. The Hub provides physical research infrastructure and real-world data; universities provide intellectual capital, research labor (GRAs), and grant-writing capacity. IP generated through these partnerships is governed by standard Material Transfer Agreements and research use licenses, with formal licensing deferred to Year 3+.

## 2. GVSU Capstone Integration

### 2.1 Partner Units
- **GVSU Brooks College of Interdisciplinary Studies**: Emphasis on applied learning, community engagement, interdisciplinary senior seminars. Ideal for systems-thinking capstones integrating food science, environmental policy, and social equity.
- **GVSU Padnos College of Engineering & Computing**: Mechanical, electrical, and biomedical engineering seniors seeking real-world design-build projects. Scope: sensor networks, automation, energy optimization.
- **GVSU CLAS — Biology and Environmental Studies**: Senior thesis students can use Hub as field/lab site for plant physiology, pollinator ecology, or ecosystem services research.
- **GVSU Seidman College of Business**: Supply chain, agribusiness, and social enterprise capstone tracks.

### 2.2 Capstone Program Structure

| Phase | Timing | Activity |
|---|---|---|
| **Partner Identification** | August–September | Hub Education Coordinator meets with GVSU capstone coordinators; propose 4–6 project prompts |
| **Project Scoping** | September–October | Faculty advisor + Hub staff co-develop project charter; define deliverable, timeline, data access |
| **Active Project** | October–April (academic year) | Students conduct research/design at Hub (1 half-day/week on-site) |
| **Deliverable Review** | April | Hub staff review and provide feedback on draft deliverable |
| **Final Presentation** | April–May | Students present at Hub to Campus Design Council; deliverables shared with Hub |
| **IP Disposition** | May | If patentable IP identified, both parties notified; formal process initiated per MOU |

### 2.3 Project Prompt Examples (Year 1)
1. **Sensor Network Design**: Design and prototype a low-cost IoT environmental monitoring system for a 10,000 sq ft greenhouse (temperature, humidity, CO₂, PAR). (Padnos Engineering)
2. **Food Security Equity Analysis**: Analyze the distribution and accessibility of CEA-grown produce in Grand Rapids food deserts; develop a community-supported CSA model. (Brooks College / CLAS)
3. **Pollinator Health Dashboard**: Develop a beehive monitoring protocol and data visualization system integrating Varroa counts, honey production, and forage maps. (Biology/CLAS)
4. **Workforce Impact Study**: Survey CEA Operations Certificate completers at 6 months post-graduation; measure employment rate, wage change, and reported skill transfer. (Social Sciences / Seidman)
5. **Lifecycle Assessment**: Conduct an LCA comparing the environmental footprint of Hub-grown lettuce vs. field-grown California lettuce transported to West Michigan. (Environmental Studies / Engineering)

### 2.4 MOU Framework — GVSU Partnership
- Hub provides: physical access, data sharing, staff mentorship (2–4 hrs/semester per project), modest stipend for Hub staff time
- GVSU provides: faculty supervision, academic credit for students, inclusion of Hub in grant applications as research site
- IP: jointly owned if generated by student; Hub retains license to use in operations; student retains right to publish
- Fee: $500/project/semester for Hub lab use; 4–8 projects/year = $2,000–$4,000/year earned

## 3. MSU AgBioResearch Sponsored Research Integration

### 3.1 MSU Research Landscape
MSU AgBioResearch ([agbioresearch.msu.edu](https://agbioresearch.msu.edu)) encompasses 300+ faculty conducting food and agricultural research. Within the Department of Horticulture, key CEA researchers include:
- **Dr. Erik Runkle**: Environmental physiology of specialty plants in controlled environments; LED lighting research; Project GREEEN PI for 20+ years ([canr.msu.edu](https://www.canr.msu.edu/news/controlled-environment-agriculture-research-sees-innovation-through-project-greeen))
- **Dr. Roberto Lopez**: CEA HERB project PI ($3.4M USDA SCRI); indoor production of culinary herbs; light and nutrient interactions ([canr.msu.edu](https://www.canr.msu.edu/news/msu-led-team-studying-expansion-of-controlled-environment-culinary-herb-production-across-us))
- **Dr. Simone Valle de Souza**: Horticulture economics; profitability of indoor farming; part of OptimIA consortium
- **To-Be-Named GVSU Faculty**: Biology or Biomedical Science faculty for food safety sensor/IoT research; Environmental Studies for workforce outcomes

### 3.2 Sponsored Research Model

The Hub functions as a **research satellite site** for MSU AgBioResearch. The formal relationship is structured as a **Cooperative Research and Development Agreement (CRADA)** or **Research Use Agreement (RUA)** with the following terms:

| Element | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Hub Provides** | Dedicated research grow bays (4 × 100 sq ft); environmental control; data collection support; harvest logistics; community access for outreach component |
| **MSU Provides** | PI leadership; GRA labor; experimental design; grant writing (Hub listed as project site); equipment (if grant-funded) |
| **Overhead Recovery** | Hub receives 30–40% F&A (facilities and administrative cost) recovery on sponsored project budget; approx. $12,000–$32,000 per $40K–$80K pilot |
| **IP Rights** | Michigan IP Transfer Agreement governing joint inventions; Hub retains royalty-free license for operational use; licensing revenue shared 50/50 after patent prosecution costs |
| **Publication Rights** | MSU retains right to publish; Hub receives 60-day pre-publication review; no veto right unless trade secret IP at risk |
| **Duration** | 12–18 months per pilot; renewable |

### 3.3 Pathway to Larger Grants
The sponsored research model is designed explicitly as a **pipeline to competitive federal grants**:

```
Hub Pilot ($40–80K, internal or small grant)
        ↓
MDARD SCBGP ($75–125K) or USDA NIFA AFRI Small Grant ($100–200K)
        ↓
USDA SCRI SREP ($226K–$6.2M) — Hub as project site, MSU as lead
        ↓
USDA SCRI CAP ($2M–$10M) — multi-institution consortium
        ↓
IP commercialization & licensing (Y3+)
```

Precedent: MSU's OptimIA project followed exactly this pathway from Project GREEEN seed funding → $2.7M USDA SCRI ([canr.msu.edu](https://www.canr.msu.edu/news/controlled-environment-agriculture-research-sees-innovation-through-project-greeen)). The Hub can replicate this trajectory with 3–4 years of investment.

## 5. Technology Transfer & IP Roadmap

### Year 1 (Pilot Phase): No licensing revenue expected
- Focus: establish research use agreements, conduct baseline experiments, produce publishable data
- IP audit: identify potential protectable inventions; engage MSU Technology Transfer or GVSU IP office
- Cost: IP counsel retainer ~$5,000 for scoping

### Year 2 (Provisional Patent Filing): Possible licensing discussions begin
- If Y1 data shows novel efficacy (e.g., specific LED spectrum protocol producing measurable nutritional enhancement), file provisional patent application (~$3,000–$5,000)
- Begin outreach to CEA equipment manufacturers or nutrient companies as potential licensees
- Realistic licensing revenue: $0–$25,000 (option agreements only)

### Year 3+ (Active Licensing): Commercial licensing and royalty stream
- License provisional protocol to 2–3 commercial CEA operators nationally
- Annual royalty: 3–5% of gross revenue attributable to licensed method; realistic range $50,000–$150,000/year if 2 licensees adopt
- Co-star scenario: Hub becomes regional CEA research hub with ongoing grant support, licensing income, and national recognition

### Year 3+ Revenue Scenario (Council-Aligned "Realistic Case")
| Revenue Source | Y3 Annual Estimate |
|---|---|
| Sponsored research overhead recovery (4 pilots) | $128,000–$256,000 |
| IP licensing (2 licenses × $50K avg) | $100,000 |
| GVSU capstone fees | $4,000 |
| Grant-funded GRA stipends (pass-through) | ~$80,000 |
| **Total Higher Ed / R&D Revenue** | **~$312,000–$440,000** |

*This is the scenario that justifies the pro-forma's $1.1M claim — but only if pilot data is strong and licensing pipeline is actively managed. Y1 realistic is $60K–$80K (overhead on 1 pilot + capstone fees).*

## 6. Governance & Coordination

### Joint Research Committee
- **Composition:** Hub Executive Director, MSU PI representative, GVSU faculty lead, Hub Education Coordinator, GRCC liaison
- **Frequency:** Quarterly; ad hoc for grant submissions
- **Responsibilities:** Review pilot data, authorize new projects, manage IP disclosures, approve publications, coordinate grant submissions

### Conflict of Interest Policy
Hub staff participating as co-investigators or co-authors must disclose their Hub employment in all publications. Any commercialization arrangement involving Hub staff must be approved by both the Hub's board and the relevant university's COI office.
