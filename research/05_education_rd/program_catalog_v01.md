**Prepared by:** Prof. Elena Cruz, Curriculum Developer, CEA Campus Design Council

**Date:** 2026 | Temperature 0.5

## Executive Summary

The CEA Agritech Hub Education Platform operates across three mutually reinforcing layers: K-12 experiential learning (anchored in Kent ISD's science and CTE pipeline), community college workforce credentials (GRCC + West Michigan Works!), and higher education research integration (GVSU/MSU capstone and sponsored-research partnerships). Together, these layers serve approximately 1,200–1,500 learners annually at full build-out, generate earned revenue through program fees and grants, and create a talent pipeline for both the Hub's own operations and West Michigan's broader agritech sector.

The three-year trajectory shifts the revenue base from grant-dependent (Y1: ~$350K) toward a diversified mix of earned income, licensing, and institutional partnerships (Y3+: ~$900K–$1.1M).

## Layer 1: K-12 Experiential Learning

### Program Description
Structured field-trip and classroom-extension programming for grades 3–12, with deepest integration at the middle-school level (8th grade "River → Plate" unit) aligned to NGSS and Michigan Science Standards. Secondary pathway: FFA/Kent ISD CTE Agriculture articulation for high school students.

### Program Offerings

| Offering | Target Audience | Duration | Fee | Annual Cohorts | Annual Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Discovery Day Field Trip (Gr. 3–5) | Elementary | 3 hrs | $12/student | 40 groups × 25 students | $12,000 |
| Ecosystem Explorer Lab (Gr. 6–8) | Middle School | 6 hrs | $18/student | 50 groups × 28 students | $25,200 |
| River → Plate 6-Week Unit | 8th Grade | 6 weeks (in-class + 2 site visits) | $22/student | 15 classrooms × 28 | $9,240 |
| High School CEA Internship (half-day) | Gr. 9–12, FFA | Semester | $0 (CTE-funded) | 20 students | $8,000 (CTE allocation) |
| Teacher Professional Development | K-12 Teachers | 2-day workshop | $150/teacher | 2 cohorts × 20 | $6,000 |

**Layer 1 Annual Earned Revenue (Y2+):** ~$60,440
**Layer 1 Grant Revenue (Y1):** ~$35,000 (Kent ISD CTE Perkins + MDARD SCBGP)

### Standards Alignment
- NGSS: MS-LS2-1, MS-LS2-3, MS-ESS3-3, MS-ETS1-1 through ETS1-4
- Michigan Science Standards: Fully aligned (Michigan adopted NGSS with minor modifications; state standards document references NGSS framework)
- MDE CTE Agriculture, Food & Natural Resources Cluster: Animal Systems, Agribusiness Systems, Plant Systems pathways
- Common Core ELA: CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.RST.6-8.3 (follow procedures), CCSS.ELA-LITERACY.WHST.6-8.2 (informational writing)
- Common Core Math: CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.6.SP.A (statistical variability), CCSS.MATH.CONTENT.7.RP.A (proportional reasoning for yield calculations)

### Comparable Programs (Benchmarks)
- **Freight Farms campus initiatives** ([freightfarms.com](https://www.freightfarms.com/blog/sustainable-campus-initiatives)): Container farm as interactive lab; schools use hydroponic units as science learning spaces with consistent, repeatable results.
- **SUNY Morrisville CEA Greenhouse** ([morrisville.edu](https://www.morrisville.edu/facility/controlled-environment-agriculture-greenhouse)): 4,000-gallon aquaponics demonstration lab; model for K-12 field trip programming integrated with college research.
- **LaGuardia Community College Urban Farm** ([laguardia.edu](https://www.laguardia.edu/majors/sustainable-urban-agriculture/)): Outdoor and hydroponic research center used for community education, providing a community-college-anchored K-12 bridge model.

## Layer 2: Community College Workforce Credentials

### Program Description
Two stackable certificates delivered in partnership with Grand Rapids Community College (GRCC) and West Michigan Works!: a 16-week CEA Operations Certificate and an 8-week Beekeeping Technician Certificate. Programs are eligible for Michigan Reconnect tuition assistance (participants 25+) and Going PRO Talent Fund employer co-funding.

### Program Offerings

| Certificate | Partner | Duration | Credits | Tuition | Cohort Size | Annual Cohorts | Annual Revenue |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| CEA Operations Certificate | GRCC + WMW! | 16 weeks | 12 credits | $2,400/student | 20 | 2 | $96,000 |
| Beekeeping Technician Certificate | GRCC + WMW! | 8 weeks | 6 credits | $1,200/student | 16 | 2 | $38,400 |
| Continuing Ed / Employer Module (custom) | WMW! Going PRO | 2 days | Non-credit | $350/person | 12 | 4 | $16,800 |

**Layer 2 Annual Earned Revenue (Y2+):** ~$151,200
**Michigan Reconnect offset:** reduces net tuition burden per student; revenue flows through GRCC enrollment
**Going PRO Talent Fund:** employer reimbursement of up to 50% of training costs for incumbent workers

### Standards Alignment
- MDE CTE Agriculture, Food & Natural Resources: Plant Systems, Agribusiness Systems, Environmental Service Systems pathway standards
- MSU Extension CEA curriculum benchmarks (OptimIA project outputs)
- CEA Works / GLASE framework: Head Grower and Assistant Grower competencies (Cornell University/OSU, USDA NIFA-funded) ([glase.org](https://glase.org/resources/ceaworks/))

### Comparable Programs (Benchmarks)
- **CEA Works (GLASE/Cornell/OSU)** ([glase.org/resources/ceaworks](https://glase.org/resources/ceaworks/)): USDA NIFA-funded online certificates for Head Growers, Assistant Growers, CEA Farm Business Managers; four courses including crop production overview, plant physiology, root zone management, business planning. Direct curriculum model.
- **LaGuardia CC Sustainable Urban Agriculture AS** ([laguardia.edu](https://www.laguardia.edu/majors/sustainable-urban-agriculture/)): 18-credit SUA track with hydroponics, soil science, urban farm lab. Associate degree model for potential articulation.
- **Ohio State CEA Specialization** ([hcs.osu.edu/cea](https://hcs.osu.edu/cea)): Greenhouse management, hydroponics, fruit/vegetable production, engineering principles embedded in HCS major. Provides 4-year articulation target for GRCC graduates.

## Layer 3: Higher Education Research Integration

### Program Description
Capstone project hosting, sponsored-research pilot agreements, and co-mentorship arrangements with Grand Valley State University (GVSU) and Michigan State University (MSU AgBioResearch). Faculty from GVSU CLAS/Brooks College and MSU Department of Horticulture serve as research leads; the Hub provides research infrastructure (grow space, sensors, data) in exchange for investigator-led grant applications listing Hub as project site.

### Program Offerings

| Offering | Partner | Duration | Annual Volume | Revenue Model |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Capstone Project Hosting | GVSU Brooks/CLAS | 1 semester | 6–10 projects | $500/project (lab use fee) = $3,000–$5,000 |
| Sponsored Research Pilot (see pilots memo) | MSU AgBioResearch / GVSU | 12–18 months | 2 pilots Y1, 4 Y3 | $40,000–$80,000/pilot overhead recovery |
| Graduate Student Research Assistantship | MSU/GVSU | Academic year | 2 GRAs | Grant-funded |
| IP Licensing (deferred Y3+) | TBD tech transfer | — | — | Royalties, milestone payments |

**Layer 3 Sponsored Research Revenue (Y1):** ~$40,000–$80,000 (1 pilot)
**Layer 3 Sponsored Research Revenue (Y3+):** ~$160,000–$320,000 (4 pilots)
**Layer 3 IP Licensing Revenue (Y3+):** TBD; realistic range $0–$150,000/year depending on patent prosecution and market validation

### Comparable Programs (Benchmarks)
- **MSU AgBioResearch / Project GREEEN** ([canr.msu.edu](https://www.canr.msu.edu/news/controlled-environment-agriculture-research-sees-innovation-through-project-greeen)): 25+ years of CEA research; $2.7M USDA SCRI award (OptimIA); $3.4M SCRI HERB project led by Dr. Roberto Lopez. Demonstrates feasibility of $40–80K pilot → $3M+ SCRI follow-on.
- **Cornell GLASE consortium**: Multi-institution CEA research integrating Cornell, OSU, SUNY Broome, Agritecture. Model for Hub's consortium-building strategy.
- **Ohio State CEAC Training Programs** ([ohceac.osu.edu](https://ohceac.osu.edu/CEBPI-Training-Programs)): Customized group and one-on-one industry training; strawberry production under CEA. Demonstrates fee-for-service research training model.

### Michigan Science Standards Note
Michigan's K-12 Science Standards ([michigan.gov](https://www.michigan.gov/-/media/Project/Websites/mde/Literacy/Content-Standards/Science_Standards.pdf)) are structurally aligned with NGSS; Michigan was a Lead State in NGSS development ([nextgenscience.org](https://www.nextgenscience.org/lead-state-michigan)). All NGSS Performance Expectations cited above are directly applicable in Michigan classrooms. Michigan requires three science credits for graduation including one biology and one chemistry or physics.

### MDE CTE Agriculture Pathways
The Michigan CTE Agriculture, Food & Natural Resources cluster ([michigan.gov MDE CTE](https://www.michigan.gov/mde/services/octe/educators/cte-instructional-resources-for-all-programs/career-clusters/agriculture)) encompasses the following pathways relevant to the Hub:
- **Plant Systems** (Horticulture, controlled environment production)
- **Agribusiness Systems** (farm economics, enterprise management)
- **Environmental Service Systems** (water quality, nutrient management)
- **Food Products & Processing Systems** (post-harvest, food safety, value-added)

The Hub's certificate programs align with Plant Systems and Environmental Service Systems competencies.
