# R&D Revenue Ladder — Honest Y1 / Y2 / Y3 Analysis
## Separating Grants / Pilots / Licensing
**Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub — CEA Campus Design Council**
**Prepared by:** Prof. Elena Cruz, CEA Campus Design Council
**Council Context:** Pro-forma claims $1.1M Year-1 R&D + licensing revenue. Council view: Y1 realistic R&D revenue is $0.35M (grant-funded pilots only), with true IP licensing deferred to Y3+.
**Date:** 2026
**Frameworks:** Five S + TRACE + Co-Star (analytical rigor mode)

---

## 1. Executive Position

The pro-forma's Year-1 projection of $1.1M in combined R&D and licensing revenue is not achievable. It conflates three fundamentally different revenue streams — grants, sponsored research, and IP licensing — each of which operates on a different timeline, requires different prerequisites, and carries different risk profiles. This memo disaggregates those streams, establishes a credible phased revenue ladder, and specifies the conditions that must be met for each step up.

**Year 1 Realistic R&D Revenue: $320,000–$450,000** (grants only)
**Year 2 Realistic R&D Revenue: $550,000–$800,000** (grants + sponsored research recovery)
**Year 3 Realistic R&D Revenue: $850,000–$1,300,000** (grants + sponsored research + early licensing discussions)

The $1.1M figure is a Year-3 scenario, achievable only if Y1 and Y2 milestones are fully executed.

---

## 2. Revenue Stream Taxonomy

### Stream A: Grant Revenue (Non-Dilutive, Non-Repayable)
**Definition:** Competitive and formula grants from government and philanthropic funders
**Prerequisites:** Eligible applicant status, project design matching funder priorities, application preparation
**Timeline:** 3–12 months from application to award; 1–3 year project periods
**Risk Level:** Medium (competitive; 20–40% success rate for first-time applicants)
**Revenue Recognition:** When awarded and work is performed; typically quarterly reimbursements

### Stream B: Sponsored Research Overhead Recovery (Earned)
**Definition:** Facilities and Administrative (F&A) cost recovery on externally funded research conducted at Hub
**Prerequisites:** Formal Research Use Agreement with university partner; grant awarded naming Hub as project site; negotiated F&A rate (typically 25–40% of direct costs)
**Timeline:** Y2+ (university PI must first win grant; Hub is site, not lead)
**Risk Level:** Medium-High (depends on university PI grant success)
**Revenue Recognition:** Quarterly from university to Hub per research agreement

### Stream C: Sponsored Research Direct Awards (Earned)
**Definition:** Hub as direct sub-awardee or prime on smaller grants (MDARD SCBGP, USDA VAPG, Going PRO)
**Prerequisites:** Hub as eligible applicant (agricultural producer, educational institution, or employer)
**Timeline:** Y1 (MDARD SCBGP, VAPG) and Y2 (AFRI sub-award)
**Risk Level:** Low-Medium (smaller, less competitive programs)

### Stream D: IP Licensing (Royalty Revenue)
**Definition:** License fees, milestone payments, and running royalties from commercialized research outputs
**Prerequisites:** (1) Pilot research must produce novel, demonstrably efficacious protocol or technology; (2) Patent prosecution (18–24 months minimum); (3) Technology transfer negotiation; (4) Licensee must adopt and report sales
**Timeline:** Y3 minimum for first royalty payment; Y4–Y5 for meaningful recurring income
**Risk Level:** High (most academic IP produces <$50K/year; outlier licenses can be much larger)

## 5. Key Risk Factors and Mitigations

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|---|
| MDARD SCBGP not awarded in Y1 | 30% | -$100K revenue | Submit strongest possible application; have VAPG as backup; apply to GRCF simultaneously |
| MSU PI does not win AFRI in Y2 | 50% | -$80K–$160K | Simultaneously develop GVSU PI relationship; pursue SCRI SREP directly |
| Rural eligibility fails for REAP (Grand Rapids >50K population) | 40% | -$100K–$200K | Confirm with USDA RD Michigan state office before investing in application |
| Pilot research produces inconclusive data (no licensable IP) | 50% | IP licensing $0 in Y3 | Frame IP licensing as upside scenario; do not include in financial model baseline |
| Going PRO Talent Fund defunded (politically vulnerable program) | 25% | -$15K–$40K | WIOA ITA and OJT programs are more durable backups |
| GVSU or MSU faculty lead unavailable | 35% | Delays pilot by 1 year | Begin faculty recruitment in Q1 Y1; have backup faculty contacts identified |

---

## 6. Council Recommendation

**Do not use $1.1M as Year 1 R&D revenue in the pro-forma.** The credible Y1 figure is $285,000–$515,000. The $1.1M figure is defensible only as a Year 3 scenario under favorable conditions (strong grant pipeline execution, successful SCRI application, and at least one licensing agreement).

**Revised Pro-Forma Recommendation:**
| Year | Conservative | Realistic | Optimistic |
|---|---|---|---|
| Y1 | $200,000 | $350,000 | $515,000 |
| Y2 | $500,000 | $800,000 | $1,100,000 |
| Y3 | $750,000 | $1,100,000 | $1,700,000 |

This ladder preserves the $1.1M headline for investor communications as a realistic Year 3 target — which is credible and defensible — while ensuring the Hub's operating plan is not built on Y1 revenue that cannot materialize.
