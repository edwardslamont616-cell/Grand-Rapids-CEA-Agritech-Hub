# CERTIFICATE 1: CEA Operations Certificate
## 16-Week Program | 12 Credits | Stackable toward GRCC AAS

### Program Identity

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Program Title** | CEA Operations Certificate |
| **Institution** | Grand Rapids Community College (GRCC) in partnership with Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub |
| **Duration** | 16 weeks (one semester) |
| **Credits** | 12 credit hours |
| **Delivery Mode** | Hybrid: 40% online (asynchronous + synchronous), 60% lab/site-based at Hub |
| **Schedule** | Tuesday/Thursday 9–11:30 AM (lab) + self-paced online modules |
| **Tuition** | $2,400 total (~$200/credit) |
| **Funding Pathways** | Michigan Reconnect (age 25+, tuition-free for eligible learners); Going PRO Talent Fund (employer reimbursement); Title I / WIOA pathway for qualifying adults |
| **Credential** | GRCC Certificate + CEA Hub "Certified CEA Operator" badge (digital credential, NCES-aligned) |
| **Program Lead** | Hub Education Coordinator + GRCC Agriculture Adjunct Instructor |

### Program Outcomes
Upon successful completion, graduates will be able to:
1. Set up, calibrate, and maintain hydroponic systems (DWC, NFT, Kratky) following commercial SOPs
2. Monitor and adjust plant nutrient solutions (pH 5.5–6.5; EC by crop species) using digital instruments
3. Interpret Daily Light Integral (DLI) data and adjust LED photoperiod schedules to optimize yield
4. Execute an Integrated Pest Management (IPM) protocol including identification of common CEA pests and approved management strategies
5. Operate post-harvest handling equipment and maintain food safety records under GAP/FSMA standards
6. Collect, enter, and interpret basic crop performance data using a farm management platform
7. Explain the energy systems powering a CEA facility and calculate energy cost per kg of produce
8. Identify professional pathways: Head Grower, Assistant Grower, CEA Farm Manager

### Alignment
- **MDE CTE Agriculture, Food & Natural Resources:** Plant Systems pathway; Environmental Service Systems pathway
- **CEA Works / GLASE Framework** ([glase.org/resources/ceaworks](https://glase.org/resources/ceaworks/)): Assistant Grower and Head Grower competencies
- **MSU OptimIA / CEA HERB research outputs** ([canr.msu.edu](https://www.canr.msu.edu/news/controlled-environment-agriculture-research-sees-innovation-through-project-greeen)): LEDs, nutrient protocols, food safety

### Course Schedule by Week

| Week | Module | Topics | Lab / Site Activity | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Orientation & Systems Overview | CEA history, systems taxonomy (greenhouse, vertical farm, container), Tour of Hub | Hub facility orientation + safety training | Safety quiz; intake skills assessment |

| 4 | Hydroponic Systems II | NFT, vertical towers, aeroponics overview; pros/cons matrix | NFT channel setup; plant transplant protocol | Systems comparison matrix |
| 5 | Nutrient Management I | Macro/micronutrients, N-P-K for leafy greens vs. fruiting crops; mixing stock solutions | Solution prep lab: mix A+B concentrate; measure EC and pH | Nutrient solution log |
| 6 | Nutrient Management II | Deficiency and toxicity identification, pH effects on uptake, recirculating vs. run-to-waste | Deficiency identification from plant photos + live plants; adjust solution | Deficiency ID quiz |
| 7 | Precision Lighting | PPFD, DLI, photoperiod, spectrum (R:B:FR ratios); LED vs. HPS economics | Light measurement lab: measure PPFD at multiple canopy heights using quantum flux meter | DLI calculation worksheet |
| 8 | Climate Control | Temperature, humidity (VPD), CO₂ enrichment, air circulation; HVAC basics | Walk-through of Hub climate control system; read HVAC data dashboard | Climate parameter scenario exercise |
| 9 | **Midterm Week** | Review Weeks 1–8; individual skills assessment | Practical: set up and calibrate a complete hydroponic station from scratch | Midterm practical (40%) + written quiz (60%) |
| 10 | Integrated Pest Management | CEA pest profiles (fungus gnats, aphids, spider mites, powdery mildew); biological controls; exclusion strategies | Scout and identify pests using hub production space (with instructor); complete IPM log | IPM scouting report |
| 11 | Food Safety & Post-Harvest | FSMA Produce Safety Rule basics; GAP principles; harvest, cleaning, packaging; cold chain | Harvest protocol: harvest, wash, weigh, package lettuce; temperature log; label | Food safety compliance checklist |
| 12 | Energy Systems & Sustainability | Micro-hydro and solar integration; energy balance calculations; carbon footprint of CEA vs. field | Energy audit exercise: calculate kWh/kg lettuce for Hub system | Energy cost per kg calculation |
| 13 | Farm Data & Business Basics | Yield tracking, cost of production, pricing, break-even analysis; farm management software intro | Enter 4-week production data into spreadsheet; calculate cost/head | Production data analysis report |
| 14 | Specialty Crops & Diversification | Herbs, microgreens, tomatoes, strawberries; market positioning; value-added products | Harvest microgreens; price comparison exercise (Hub vs. retail) | Market positioning one-pager |
| 15 | Career Pathways & Capstone Prep | Head Grower vs. Farm Manager career trajectories; resume workshop; site visit to 2nd CEA operation in West MI | Industry partner guest speaker; resume draft | Resume draft + cover letter |
| 16 | **Capstone Week** | Capstone project presentations; certificate ceremony | Individual or team capstone: 10-minute presentation of an operational improvement proposal for the Hub | Capstone rubric (see below) |

### Capstone Project Description
Students propose and present one operational improvement to the Hub's CEA system, supported by at least 4 weeks of their own collected data. The proposal must include:
- Problem statement with supporting data
- Proposed solution with justification from course readings and lab experience
- Feasibility analysis (cost, labor, timeline)
- Anticipated outcome (yield improvement, cost reduction, food safety enhancement, or other measurable metric)

### Required Texts & Resources
- CEA Works online modules (GLASE/Cornell, USDA NIFA-funded): Overview of Crop Production in CEA, Plant Physiology of CEA Crops, Root Zone Environment Management ([glase.org](https://glase.org/resources/ceaworks/))
- MSU Extension CEA grower fact sheets (free, canr.msu.edu)
- Hub-developed SOP binder (provided)
- FSMA Produce Safety Alliance Grower Training materials (USDA-funded, free)

### Student Enrollment Profile (Target)
Michigan Reconnect-eligible adults (25+) seeking career transition; returning citizens (second-chance pathway); veterans; recent high school graduates interested in food systems; GRCC enrolled students seeking elective credits toward AAS in Agriculture/Horticulture.

# CERTIFICATE 2: Beekeeping Technician Certificate
## 8-Week Program | 6 Credits | Stackable

### Program Identity

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Program Title** | Beekeeping Technician Certificate |
| **Institution** | Grand Rapids Community College (GRCC) in partnership with Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub Apiary |
| **Duration** | 8 weeks |
| **Credits** | 6 credit hours |
| **Delivery Mode** | Hybrid: 30% online (asynchronous), 70% apiary lab at Hub |
| **Schedule** | Saturday 8:00 AM–12:00 PM (apiary lab) + self-paced online modules (2 hr/week) |
| **Tuition** | $1,200 total |
| **Funding Pathways** | Michigan Reconnect (eligible if taken alongside CEA Certificate); Going PRO (for honey/food businesses); WMW! sector training |
| **Credential** | GRCC Certificate + EAS (Eastern Apicultural Society) Certified Beekeeper prep alignment |
| **Program Lead** | Hub Apiary Specialist (Amira Bloom or successor) + GRCC instructor of record |
| **Safety Note** | Students must complete allergy screening before enrollment; EpiPen protocol in effect; PPE provided |

### Program Outcomes
Upon successful completion, graduates will be able to:
1. Safely conduct a full hive inspection using appropriate PPE and smoker technique
2. Identify and record colony health indicators: queen presence, brood pattern, honey stores, Varroa infestation level
3. Perform alcohol wash and/or sugar roll Varroa mite count and interpret results against established thresholds
4. Apply approved Varroa management treatments (oxalic acid vaporization, Apiguard/thymol) according to label and regulatory requirements
5. Describe the annual colony cycle (winter cluster, spring buildup, swarm season, fall prep) and seasonal management calendar
6. Harvest and process honey to commercial food safety standards (moisture content, filtering, labeling)
7. Explain pollination biology as an ecosystem service and the role of managed bees in CEA crop production
8. Communicate basic bee biology, hive life, and food system connections to a public (non-expert) audience

### Course Schedule by Week

| Week | Module | Topics | Apiary Lab Activity | Assessment |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Bee Biology & Colony Structure | Caste system (queen, workers, drones); anatomy; life cycle; eusocial behavior | Classroom/online only (safety orientation; equipment fitting; PPE practice) | Safety quiz; allergy clearance confirmed |
| 2 | Equipment & Hive Types | Langstroth hive anatomy; frames, supers, bottom boards; tools (hive tool, smoker); PPE components | First hive opening: smoke and inspect with instructor; read frames | Equipment identification quiz |
| 3 | Colony Inspection | What to look for: queen, capped/open brood, eggs, honey, pollen; brood disease signs (AFB, EFB, sacbrood) | Full inspection of 2 hives; photograph 3 frames; record data on inspection sheet | Inspection data sheet; 5-question ID quiz |
| 4 | Varroa Mite Management | Varroa destructor biology; economic threshold (≥2% infestation); alcohol wash vs. sugar roll protocol; IPM treatment options | Conduct alcohol wash on one hive; calculate infestation percentage; determine treatment decision | Varroa mite count lab report |
| 5 | Seasonal Colony Management | Spring inspection and buildup; swarm prevention (splitting, adding supers); summer management; fall prep and winter cluster formation | Spring or fall inspection (seasonally appropriate); assist with or observe a hive split | Management decision memo (1 page) |
| 6 | Honey Harvest & Processing | Timing a honey harvest; uncapping; extraction (centrifuge); filtering; Brix measurement; moisture and food safety; labeling requirements (Michigan Cottage Food Law) | Extract and filter honey from a prepared super; Brix test; label a jar | Honey processing checklist; Brix reading |
| 7 | Pollination Services & CEA Integration | Pollination biology (flower anatomy, nectar/pollen rewards); managed bee services in greenhouse/CEA; wild bee conservation; urban beekeeping regulations | Observe bees foraging in Hub greenhouse (or nearby outdoor site); tally visitation rates | Pollination observation report |
| 8 | **Capstone & Public Engagement** | Science communication; interpretive tour design; career pathways (commercial beekeeper, inspector, ag educator, honey brand) | Lead a 15-minute guided apiary tour for a small public group; fielding visitor questions | Tour performance rubric; written reflection |

### Assessment Distribution
| Component | Weight |
|---|---|
| Safety quiz (Week 1) | 5% |
| Weekly inspection data sheets (Weeks 2–5) | 25% |
| Varroa mite count lab report | 15% |
| Management decision memo | 15% |
| Honey processing checklist + Brix data | 15% |
| Pollination observation report | 10% |
| Capstone: public tour performance | 15% |

### Stackable Pathway
Students who complete both certificates (CEA Operations + Beekeeping Technician) earn a **Pollinator-Integrated CEA Specialist** designation (18 credits total). This 18-credit block can articulate into GRCC's AAS in Environmental Studies or toward a 4-year degree in Horticulture at GVSU or Michigan State.

### Required Materials
- Hub-provided PPE (veil, gloves, coveralls) — included in tuition
- Course reader: selected chapters from "The Beekeeper's Handbook" (Sammataro & Avitabile) + Hub SOP binder
- EAS Certified Beekeeper study guide (recommended, self-purchase ~$15)
- Michigan Apiary Laws summary sheet (free, Michigan Department of Agriculture)
