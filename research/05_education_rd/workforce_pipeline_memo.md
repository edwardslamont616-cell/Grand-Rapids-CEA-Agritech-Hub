## 1. Purpose

This memo documents the workforce pipeline strategy for the Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub's education and employment programming, with particular attention to three underserved learner populations: (1) adults seeking career transitions via Michigan Reconnect, (2) incumbent workers upskilling through West Michigan Works! employer services, and (3) returning citizens and second-chance job seekers for whom the Hub's founder (Lamont Edwards) has expressed personal and organizational commitment.

The strategy is organized around three pathways that are financially efficient (leveraging public funding rather than Hub operating budget), strategically aligned (each pathway serves Hub hiring needs and community mission), and scalable (each can grow without proportional Hub staff increases).

## 2. West Michigan Works! (WMW!) Integration

### Program Overview
West Michigan Works! ([westmiworks.org](https://www.westmiworks.org)) is the regional Workforce Development Board serving Kent, Ionia, Mecosta, Montcalm, Newaygo, and Osceola counties. It administers WIOA Title I adult and dislocated worker funds, the Michigan Going PRO Talent Fund for employers, Michigan Reconnect for adults seeking certificates, and sector-specific training partnerships.

### Relevant WMW! Programs for Hub

| Program | Description | Hub Application |
|---|---|---|
| **Registered Apprenticeship / OJT** | On-the-Job Training agreements; employer receives up to 50% wage reimbursement during training period | Hub registers as OJT employer; new CEA Operator hires receive up to 6 months wage subsidy while completing on-site training |
| **Individual Training Account (ITA)** | WIOA-funded voucher ($3,000–$8,000) enabling eligible adults to enroll in approved training programs | GRCC CEA Operations Certificate listed as eligible; WMW! clients use ITA to cover tuition; Hub recruits from WMW! roster |
| **Going PRO Talent Fund** | Michigan employer grant for workforce training (Cycle 1 Oct 30; Cycle 2 Apr 24) ([michigan.gov/leo](https://www.michigan.gov/leo/bureaus-agencies/wd/programs-services/going-pro-talent-fund)) | Hub applies as employer; funds training for 10–15 Hub and partner-employer hires per year through GRCC; applications through WMW! |
| **Sector Partnership Engagement** | WMW! convenes employer tables by sector; Hub anchors a CEA/food systems sector partnership | Hub + 3–5 other local agritech employers form a sector table; co-develop competency frameworks; strengthen Going PRO application |
| **Pre-Apprenticeship / Youth Programs** | Programs for 16–24-year-olds; work-based learning; industry tours | Hub hosts WMW! youth cohorts for monthly 2-hour site visits; pipeline to GRCC certificate |

## 3. Michigan Reconnect Integration

### Program Overview
Michigan Reconnect ([michigan.gov/reconnect](https://www.michigan.gov/reconnect)) provides tuition-free community college for Michigan residents who are:
- Age 25+ (Reconnect standard); Reconnect Expansion for ages 21–24 was available until December 2024 but new expansion applications are closed for non-prior applicants
- Michigan residents for ≥1 year
- Without an associate degree or higher
- Enrolled in an eligible Pell-approved certificate (≥16 credits) or associate degree program

### Application to Hub Programs

The GRCC **CEA Operations Certificate (12 credits)** falls just below the 16-credit threshold for standalone Michigan Reconnect eligibility. **Strategic response: expand to 16 credits** by adding one of the following:
- A 4-credit GRCC Introduction to Agriculture Business (AGR 101 equivalent) course, OR
- A 4-credit GRCC Food Safety and Processing course

This would make the certificate fully Michigan Reconnect-eligible, dramatically increasing enrollment from adults 25+ who cannot afford tuition.

The **Beekeeping Technician Certificate (6 credits)** is not independently Reconnect-eligible but can stack with the CEA Operations Certificate (18 total credits combined) to qualify.

### Marketing Reconnect to Prospective Students
- WMW! service centers actively counsel Reconnect-eligible adults; Hub's visibility at WMW! orientations critical
- GRCC's Financial Aid office processes Reconnect applications; Hub's liaison relationship with GRCC Admissions ensures CEA students are directed to Reconnect before enrollment
- Second-chance population (returning citizens): Michigan Reconnect application is straightforward and mobile-friendly; Hub case manager (or WMW! navigator) can assist with application at Hub orientation

### Reconnect Enrollment Projection

| Certificate | Reconnect-Eligible (if 16+ credits) | Estimated % who use Reconnect | Reconnect Students/Year (2 cohorts) |
|---|---|---|---|
| CEA Operations (expanded to 16 cr.) | Yes | 40% | 16 students |
| Beekeeping Technician (stacked, 22 cr. total) | Yes (stacked) | 25% | 8 students |

Revenue to GRCC from Reconnect: tuition reimbursed at GRCC standard rate; Hub sees increased enrollment and reduced financial barrier to completion.

## 4. Second-Chance Hiring Integration

### Background
Hub leadership, particularly Lamont Edwards, has publicly expressed commitment to second-chance employment — providing meaningful career pathways for returning citizens who face employment barriers due to criminal records. This is both a social mission and a practical talent pipeline: West Michigan has a significant returning-citizen population, many of whom are motivated, have industrial or manual skills, and are excluded from other sectors.

### Program Design: "Clean Slate → Green Career" Pathway

**Phase 1: Pre-Enrollment (4–6 weeks)**
- Coordination with Michigan Department of Corrections (MDOC) reentry staff and local reentry organizations (Degage Ministries, Access of West Michigan, Mel Trotter Ministries) to identify individuals within 90 days of release
- Orientation visit to Hub: farm tour, program overview, application process
- Assessment: basic literacy/numeracy (TABE or equivalent); hands-on aptitude (plant observation, tool use); motivation interview

**Phase 2: Supported Certificate Enrollment**
- Enroll in GRCC CEA Operations Certificate with Michigan Reconnect tuition support (if eligible) or Hub scholarship fund (if not)
- Assign peer mentor: a Hub employee who is also a Reconnect/returning-citizen program completer
- Weekly 1:1 check-in with Hub case manager or GRCC Student Support Services
- Transportation assistance: WMW! or MDOC reentry fund; Hub shuttle on lab days if needed

**Phase 3: Paid Hub Employment**
- Certificate completers offered first consideration for Hub CEA Operator and Beekeeping Technician positions
- Starting wage: $17–$20/hour, with $2/hour increase after 6-month review
- OJT agreement with WMW! provides 50% wage subsidy for first 6 months
- Clear 18-month promotion pathway: CEA Operator → Lead Grower → Operations Supervisor

**Phase 4: Career Diversification**
- Encourage completers who show aptitude for instruction to become Teaching Assistants for future certificate cohorts (paid stipend)
- Connect high performers to GVSU capstone teams or MSU pilot research projects as paid research technicians
- Support those seeking entrepreneurship with GRCC Small Business Center resources

### Legal Considerations
- Michigan's Clean Slate Act (PA 123 of 2020) allows automatic expungement of certain convictions; Hub HR policy should be aligned with Clean Slate provisions
- EEOC guidance on criminal history and hiring: Hub's policy should use individualized assessment (nature of offense, time elapsed, relevance to job duties) rather than blanket disqualification
- Federal bonding program (USDOL): Hub can request fidelity bonds for returning-citizen employees at no cost; reduces employer risk perception

### Projected Scale
- Y1: 4–6 second-chance participants in first CEA certificate cohort
- Y2: 8–12 participants; dedicated second-chance cohort if demand warrants
- Y3: second-chance pathway formally documented as a Hub program; featured in Going PRO and EPA EJG2G grant narratives
