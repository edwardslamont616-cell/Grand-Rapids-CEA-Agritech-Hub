## Executive Summary

California almond pollination is the economic backbone of US commercial beekeeping. It is also increasingly risky, expensive, and loss-prone. The 2025 average pollination fee reached **$209/colony** — but after transport, loss replacement, and feed costs, net margins are under pressure like never before. The winter 2024–25 season saw the **highest commercial colony loss on record: 40.7%**. This memo frames Michigan indoor winter storage as the complement to — not replacement for — California almond pollination, providing Michigan beekeepers a path to arrive in California with stronger, more profitable colonies.

## 1. The BEFORE: What California Almond Looks Like Today

### 1.1 Scale and Demand
California almond production occupies approximately **1.4 million acres**, with ~7% in self-fertile varieties. Non-self-fertile almonds require cross-pollination by honey bees. In 2025, an estimated **2.8 million colonies** were required for almond bloom — exceeding the US total managed stock of ~2.60–2.63 million colonies on January 1, 2025 ([USDA NASS Bee Colonies, August 2025](https://esmis.nal.usda.gov/sites/default/release-files/rn301137d/w0894985t/qn59s357h/hcny0825.pdf)).

### 1.2 Pollination Fees (2025)

| Colony Strength | Average Fee (2025) | Range | Source |
|----------------|-------------------|-------|--------|
| 4–6 frames | ~$188 | $100–$200 | [West Coast Nut, Feb 2025](https://wcngg.com/2025/02/02/economic-outlook-and-other-considerations-for-the-2025-almond-pollination-season/) |
| 7–9 frames (most common) | **$196–$209** | $100–$220 | USDA NASS / WCN |
| 10–12 frames | ~$208 | $180–$250 | WCN |
| **Average, all sizes (2025)** | **$209/colony** | — | [USDA NASS Cost of Pollination, Feb 2026](https://esmis.nal.usda.gov/sites/default/release-files/795775/cospol25.pdf) |
| Premium for ≥9-frame colony | +~6% over 8-frame | ~$12.54 extra | [USDA ARS 2020](https://www.ars.usda.gov/news-events/news/research-news/2020/helping-honey-bees-make-it-through-winter-with-early-cold-storage/) |

The 2025 fee of $209 represents a **15% increase** from 2024's $181 average ([USDA NASS Cost of Pollination](https://esmis.nal.usda.gov/sites/default/release-files/795775/cospol25.pdf)). Almond pollination generated the **highest total pollination value of any crop in Regions 6 & 7 — $364 million total in 2025**, up 3% from 2024 ([RFD-TV, February 2026](https://www.rfdtv.com/pollination-costs-shift-across-crop-regions-in-2025)).

Almond pollination remains **the single largest income source for most US commercial beekeepers** — by a wide margin, generating ~$325.8 million of the roughly $400 million in total US pollination receipts in 2024 ([USDA ERS, June 2025](http://www.ers.usda.gov/data-products/charts-of-note/chart-detail?chartId=112782)).

### 1.3 Colony Strength Requirements
- Standard contract: minimum 5 frames; 8-frame average across the orchard block
- Premium contracts: 10–12 frames; command highest fees
- Frame counts verified by third-party inspectors checking 10–15% of hives in each orchard

### 1.4 Transport Costs (Michigan to California)
A Michigan beekeeper moving hives to California faces:
- **Distance:** ~2,100–2,300 miles one way
- **Trucking cost:** Commercial bee hive transport estimates range $15–$80/hive for regional moves ([RFD-TV, April 2026](https://www.rfdtv.com/million-bee-swarm-in-tennessee-highlights-the-wild-side-of-pollinator-supply-chains)); long-haul cross-country loads significantly higher
- **Semi-load:** ~160 hives per load ([Project Apis m. Guide v2.0](https://static1.squarespace.com/static/650342507631075013d25a2c/t/664636c97fc2341121d317b8/1715877582458/Indoor+Storage+Guide+V+2.0+FINAL+WEBSITE_reduced_Optimized.pdf))
- **Two-way transport (MI to CA and back):** Estimated **$40–$120/hive round trip** depending on broker, season, fuel; 2024 USDA FSA reimbursement rate $6.60/mile/truckload ([USDA FSA ELAP Handbook, 2025](https://www.fsa.usda.gov/Internet/FSA_File/1-elap_r01_a01.pdf)) implies ~$30,500 for a 2,300-mile load of 160 hives = **$190/hive one way**, or $380 round trip — clearly illustrating why beekeepers typically sell almond pollination services to defray transport rather than just moving and returning
- **Holding yard costs in California (pre-bloom):** Additional feed, labor, Varroa re-treatment at high-density yards

### 1.5 The Loss Problem

| Metric | Value | Source |
|--------|-------|--------|
| Winter 2024–25 commercial loss (national) | **40.7%** (highest on record) | [AIA/Auburn 2025](https://apiaryinspectors.org/US-beekeeping-survey-24-25) |
| Annual commercial loss 2024–25 | **56.2%** | AIA 2025 |
| Lost almond pollination income (2025 season) | >$428 million | [American Beekeeping Federation, 2025](https://www.facebook.com/AmericanBeekeepingFederation/posts/study-results-analyzing-the-high-2025-colony-lossesin-january-of-2025-us-commerc/1239043454930380/) |
| Idaho cold storage survival (Oct intake) | 82% | [USDA ARS 2020](https://www.ars.usda.gov/news-events/news/research-news/2020/helping-honey-bees-make-it-through-winter-with-early-cold-storage/) |
| Outdoor Michigan winter 2024–25 | ~59.3% survival | AIA 2025 national avg |

California holding yards are a **major Varroa and disease transmission risk** — hundreds to thousands of colonies from multiple operations in close proximity create ideal conditions for drift-based reinfestation ([USDA Climate Hubs](https://www.climatehubs.usda.gov/hubs/northeast/topic/spending-more-time-indoors-its-whats-store-us-honey-bee-colonies)).

## 2. The AFTER: How Indoor Michigan Storage Changes the Equation

### 2.1 Survival Advantage

| Wintering Method | Survival to February | Avg Frames at Release | Qualify for Premium? | Source |
|-----------------|---------------------|----------------------|---------------------|--------|
| Outdoor Michigan (2024–25 average) | ~59% | Variable | ~60% of survivors | AIA 2025 |
| October cold storage (CEA Hub target) | **82%** | 9.3 frames avg | ~78% of survivors | USDA ARS 2020 |
| November cold storage | 76% | 8.0 frames avg | ~61% of survivors | USDA ARS / PAm Guide |
| Improvement (Oct storage vs. outdoor) | **+23 pp** | +1.3 frames | Significant increase | USDA ARS 2020 |

**For a Michigan commercial beekeeper with 500 hives:**
- Outdoor wintering: ~295 colonies survive; of those, ~60% (~177) qualify for 8-frame premium
- October indoor storage: ~410 colonies survive; of those, ~78% (~320) qualify for 8-frame premium
- **Delta: 115 more surviving colonies; 143 more premium-eligible colonies**
- Revenue impact at $209 + 6% premium (≥9 frames = ~$221): **$143 × $221 = $31,603 additional revenue** from better-quality colonies alone
- Replacement cost savings at $200/nuc × 115 saved colonies = **$23,000 saved**
- Net benefit of indoor storage before storage fee: **~$54,600**
- Storage fee at $125/hive × 500 hives = **$62,500**
- **Net premium vs. outdoor cost: competitive, especially when beekeeper-time value is included**

### 2.2 The October Advantage
[USDA ARS (2020)](https://www.ars.usda.gov/news-events/news/research-news/2020/helping-honey-bees-make-it-through-winter-with-early-cold-storage/) found that October cold storage also **eliminates one costly October Varroa miticide application**, because the broodless cold storage environment renders mites phoretic (on adult bees only), making oxalic acid vaporization at exit vastly more effective. Miticide cost savings: ~$15–$30/hive.

### 2.3 Brood Break Opportunity
The 60–90 day indoor storage period creates a **natural brood break** — all mite reproduction ceases with no capped brood. Oxalic acid treatment at exit (when broodless) achieves near-100% efficacy vs. 35–90% when brood is present. This "resets the Varroa clock" for spring build-up, giving Michigan beekeepers a colony health advantage entering the critical March–May period.

## 3. The BRIDGE: How to Use Both (Not Either/Or)

Indoor Michigan winter storage is **the preparation for California almond pollination, not a substitute for it.** The value chain for a Michigan commercial beekeeper:

```
Summer (Michigan) → Fall Varroa treatment + strength check
→ October 15: Enter CEA Hub indoor storage
→ February 15: Exit with 9+ frame colonies; broodless window for OA treatment
→ Late February: Load semis for California almond bloom
→ March 1–15: Almond pollination @ $209/hive (premium tier)
→ March–April: Return to Michigan; begin spring expansion
```

**This facility does not ask Michigan beekeepers to choose between storage and almonds.** It asks them to be better prepared for almonds — to arrive with stronger colonies, fewer losses en route, and more premium-eligible hives.

## 4. Beekeeper Contract Preferences (from Research)

[ScienceDaily / University research (October 2024)](https://www.sciencedaily.com/releases/2024/10/241015183519.htm) surveying commercial beekeepers on contract preferences found:
- Beekeepers willing to accept **$8 discount/colony** for pesticide protection in California orchards (~4% of fee)
- Beekeepers strongly value **40% prepayment before transport** as working capital guarantee
- These preferences indicate beekeepers are sophisticated financial managers who respond to risk reduction tools — exactly the value proposition of indoor storage
