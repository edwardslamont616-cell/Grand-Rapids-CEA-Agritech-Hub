## Council Summary

The six-domain research sprint validates the project's *strategic* thesis and identifies four *structural corrections* that must land in the next investor addendum, the City coordination memo, and the GitHub repo README before any further outreach. The corrections are not fatal — the campus remains investable and fundable — but they reframe two signature claims and change the sequencing of capital and permits.

**The four structural corrections:**

1. **The 400 kW in-channel microturbine as proposed is unviable.** The Lower Grand River Habitat Restoration Project closed final federal authorizations in March 2026 with [$11.03 M NRCS PL-566 funding](https://www.grandrapidsmi.gov/city-news/posts/city-of-grand-rapids-and-grand-rapids-whitewater-announce-federal-funding-approval-for-lower-grand-river-habitat-restoration-project/) and a [$14.56 M City construction contract](https://www.michiganpublic.org/transportation-infrastructure/2026-02-25/project-to-remove-dams-in-grand-river-to-begin-this-summer); construction begins summer 2026 and will remove the four low-head dams that supplied essentially all hydraulic head in the project reach. The 83% self-sufficiency claim also requires a 100% capacity factor, which is physically impossible. **Recommendation:** reposition as **"River-Assisted CEA, PV-Powered Phase 1"** with a side-channel bypass hydro concept as a Phase 2 research track post-restoration.

2. **Facility energy demand is materially higher than pro forma.** The Lighting & Loads workstream rebuilt the load schedule and found **12.18 GWh/yr under the most efficient (24 L:0 D continuous low-PPFD) strategy and 14.7 GWh/yr under conventional 16 L:8 D** — 2.9× to 3.5× the pro-forma figure of 4.205 GWh/yr. This changes the REAP framing, the WTE backup sizing, and the investor ROI math. The opportunity: the [Frontiers in Plant Science 2026 study on continuous lighting](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/plant-science/articles/10.3389/fpls.2026.1783548/full) gives a defensible path to a 21% better energy-use efficiency and 16.5% lower lighting cost per kg, which is a *real* story to tell tech/AI partners and grant reviewers.

3. **Revenue ramp must be phased.** Maya Green's 52-week production calendar puts Y1 realistic CEA+processing revenue at **$650K–$900K** (not $14.6 M) for a *single-phase* launch; Elena Cruz's R&D ladder places Y1 R&D at **$320K–$450K** (grants only; not $1.1 M); Amira Bloom's market sizing puts Y1 bee storage at **~500–800 hives, not 4,000**; Jordan Rivers' 9-line P&L hits the **$1.22 M agri-tourism target** but only if marketing and programming are operational from opening day. The honest blended Y1 revenue is **$3.2–4.5 M** for a Phase-1 campus, with $14.6 M and the $18.5 M pro-forma topline becoming a Y3–Y4 scenario once Phases 2–3 are capitalized.

4. **The GitHub repo is the connective tissue.** The 60 research files produced this sprint are immediately deployable as a `/research` folder in the [Grand-Rapids-CEA-Agritech-Hub](https://github.com/edwardslamont616-cell/Grand-Rapids-CEA-Agritech-Hub) repo. Combined with the digital-twin / AI framing already in the project, this converts the concept from "a pitch" to a **due-diligence-ready open platform** — which is exactly what will attract MSU AgBioResearch, GVSU capstones, NSF INFEWS, and the Knight/Kresge/GRCF philanthropic network.

## Per-Domain Highlights (one paragraph each)

### 1. Dr. Lena Watts — Hydro & Energy
The in-channel concept is abandoned on evidence. Five Michigan comparables (Ada 40.8%, Cascade 50.0%, Croton 46.6%, Tippy 34.8%, Hardy 35–40%) confirm a realistic CF range of 42–60%, yielding **1.47–2.10 GWh/yr**, not 3.50. USGS gage 04119000 (mean 4,110 cfs, median 3,020 cfs) supports side-channel-bypass engineering post-restoration but only with 2–4 ft of available head, requiring a supplemental diversion. Permit path touches EGLE Part 301/303, USACE Section 404, FERC 18 CFR § 4.90, ESA Section 7 for the federally-endangered snuffbox mussel, and direct NRCS PL-566 non-interference — collectively **5–7 years minimum**. Phase-1 PV+BESS is the Council-recommended substitute. Three outbound drafts are in hand: EGLE pre-application, Consumers Energy DER Level-3 inquiry, and a Grand Rapids Whitewater coordination letter that explicitly commits to no in-channel installation.

### 2. Eng. Victor Ray — Lighting & Loads
A 12-fixture benchmark library (Fluence, Signify, Heliospectra, Gavita, Current, Valoya, California LightWorks) and a zone-by-zone load schedule show installed load of 2,352 kW and annual demand of **12.18 GWh/yr under 24 L:0 D continuous** — which also captures a 21% EUE improvement versus 16 L:8 D per the [2026 Frontiers study](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/plant-science/articles/10.3389/fpls.2026.1783548/full). Michigan adopted NEC 2023 effective August 29, 2025; UL 8800 listing and GFCI at all flex-cord lighting circuits are non-negotiable. Signify GreenPower TLF (4.3 µmol/J) and Valoya LL-Series (3.2 µmol/J) are the leaders for tomato and leafy greens respectively; Fluence SPYDR 2i (2.7 µmol/J, UL 8800, IP66) is the most proven multi-tier option. A standardized fixture RFI template is ready to send to three vendors.

### 3. Dr. Maya Green — Crops
Realistic Y1 CEA revenue is **$650K–$900K**, scaling to **$2.3–$4 M by Y2** and **$4.1–$7.7 M by Y3** on the Phase-1 footprint. Saffron is repositioned from revenue driver to research/tourism asset with 2–4 season maturation; it generates $18–30 K/yr by Y3 including stigma plus visitor programming. Soy processing is correctly reclassified as food manufacturing under 21 CFR Part 117 and qualifies for up to **$200K USDA VAPG** working-capital grant (April 2026 deadline, 1:1 match). Buyer priority for Y1: Gordon Food Service (HQ Wyoming, MI — existing Square Roots partnership precedent) and Corewell Health foodservice. An 8-operator case study covers Revol Greens, Gotham Greens, BrightFarms, Little Leaf Farms, AeroFarms, Bowery (wind-down lessons), Plenty, and Square Roots Michigan. Full biological-first IPM standard of care with Koppert/Biobest/BioWorks supplier lines is specified.

### 4. Amira Bloom — Apiary
The commercial 4,000-hive target is aspirational but defensible. [MDARD](https://www.michigan.gov/mdard/animals/honey-bees) holds no registry, so market sizing used [USDA NASS 2024 (82,000 Michigan colonies)](https://esmis.nal.usda.gov/sites/default/release-files/rn301137d/w0894985t/qn59s357h/hcny0825.pdf) with 40.7% 2024–25 commercial winter loss — the highest on record ([Apiary Inspectors of America](https://apiaryinspectors.org/US-beekeeping-survey-24-25)). Basis-of-design: 38–42°F, 50–65% RH, <1,500 ppm CO₂, complete darkness, one-way biosecurity flow, 4-way pallets, 25 semi-loads in and 25 out. Intake standards: Varroa ≤ 2% alcohol wash, Nosema PCR screening, AFB/EFB visual zero-tolerance, colony strength ≥ 8 frames. California almond-pollination benchmark ($209/colony 2025, +15% YoY) positions Michigan indoor storage as **preparation for** almond rather than a substitute, with an 82% vs 59% survival delta and ~$54K net benefit for a 500-hive operation. Greenhouse tomato uses *Bombus impatiens* (Koppert Natupol, Biobest Standard, $150–200 per 7–9 wk colony) — *not* honey bees, and signage must be explicit about this.

### 5. Prof. Elena Cruz — Education & R&D
A three-layer program architecture: K-12 (a full 6-week UbD unit "River → Plate" with NGSS MS-LS2-1, MS-LS2-3, MS-ESS3-3, MS-ETS1-1 and 25 daily lesson plans), community college credentials (16-week CEA Operations Certificate, 12 credits + 8-week Beekeeping Technician, 6 credits; both eligible for Michigan Reconnect and Going PRO), and higher ed (GVSU capstone hosting with 5 project prompts + MSU AgBioResearch CRADA framework). 13-grant pipeline covers REAP, VAPG, Community Facilities, SCRI, NIFA AFRI, EPA EJ G2G, MEDC, MDARD SCBG, Going PRO (deadline **April 24, 2026** — urgent), NSF INFEWS/DISES, Kresge, Dyer-Ives, and GRCF. Five sponsored-research pilot scopes at $38–60 K each name actual MSU faculty (Runkle, Lopez, Valle de Souza) and GVSU Padnos anchors. The honest R&D ladder: **Y1 $320–450 K (grants only), Y2 $550–800 K, Y3 $850 K–1.3 M** — the $1.1 M pro-forma figure is credible as Y3, not Y1.

### 6. Jordan Rivers — Agri-Tourism & Hospitality
The $1.22 M Y1 target is achievable with a **53% blended contribution margin on $2.11 M gross** ($1.12 M contribution), validated against 10 comps (IALR, Purdue Overbeck, Downtown Market GR, Meijer Gardens, Blandford, GRCM, GRAM, JBZ, GRPM, CEAg World). Michigan has **no agritourism liability statute**, so full commercial general liability + umbrella insurance ($22–35 K/yr) is non-negotiable. The 9-line product P&L lands at: guided tours $468 K / workshops $360 K / farm-to-table dinners $375 K / corporate retreats $405 K / private events $136 K / memberships $88 K / school trips $85 K / self-guided $65 K / retail (tracked separately). A 6-month RACE-framework pre-open plan with $34–71 K budget projects **$78,350 in pre-opening revenue** from founding memberships and pre-sales. Four partnership outreach drafts are ready: Experience GR (Attraction Partner + FAM tour), Downtown Market GR (ingredient supply + cross-promo), a six-hotel concierge letter (10% referral commission), and DGRI (corridor wayfinding and World of Winter integration).
