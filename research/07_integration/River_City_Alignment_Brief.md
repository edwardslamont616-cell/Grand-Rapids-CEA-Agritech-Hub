## 1. Purpose of This Brief

This two-page brief exists to place one fact on the public record before ground breaks on the [Lower Grand River Habitat Restoration Project](https://www.grandrapidsmi.gov/city-news/posts/city-of-grand-rapids-and-grand-rapids-whitewater-announce-federal-funding-approval-for-lower-grand-river-habitat-restoration-project/) this summer:

> **The Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub will not install any in-channel hydroelectric infrastructure in the restoration reach. The project is re-scoped as a river-*adjacent*, solar-powered Phase 1 campus whose education, agri-tourism, and research programs are built around — and in service of — the restored river, not the dammed one.**

The Council asks the City, GR Whitewater, DGRI, and EGLE to treat the Hub as a **complementary downtown asset** to the restoration: a year-round educational and agri-tourism anchor that helps the public interpret the river they are about to see for the first time in 170 years.

## 2. What Changed — and Why

The Council's six-domain research sprint (65 files, 290 cited sources, committed to the [Grand-Rapids-CEA-Agritech-Hub GitHub repo](https://github.com/edwardslamont616-cell/Grand-Rapids-CEA-Agritech-Hub/tree/research/council-sprint-2026-04-23)) reached three conclusions that re-scope the energy narrative:

1. **The four low-head dams that provided hydraulic head in the project reach are being removed.** [$11.03 M NRCS PL-566 funding](https://www.grandrapidsmi.gov/city-news/posts/city-of-grand-rapids-and-grand-rapids-whitewater-announce-federal-funding-approval-for-lower-grand-river-habitat-restoration-project/) closed in March 2026, the [$14.56 M City construction contract](https://www.michiganpublic.org/transportation-infrastructure/2026-02-25/project-to-remove-dams-in-grand-river-to-begin-this-summer) is signed, and construction begins summer 2026.
2. **A 400 kW in-channel microturbine was never physically viable** in this reach. The prior pro forma implied a 100% capacity factor, which no Michigan hydro facility achieves; the five nearest comparables (Ada 40.8%, Cascade 50.0%, Croton 46.6%, Tippy 34.8%, Hardy 35–40%) establish a realistic 42–60% CF. After dam removal there is insufficient head.
3. **An in-channel installation would also be inconsistent with federal ESA Section 7 obligations** for the federally-endangered snuffbox mussel ([Epioblasma triquetra](https://ecos.fws.gov/ecp/species/7829)) whose habitat recovery is the explicit biological purpose of the restoration.

**Council conclusion:** the only honest and community-aligned posture is to decline in-channel hydro entirely and reposition the Hub's energy system as **PV + BESS Phase 1 + WTE backup**, with any future hydro concept limited to a **side-channel bypass research pilot** to be studied only *after* restoration completion and only in coordination with GR Whitewater, the City, EGLE, USACE, USFWS, and FERC.

## 3. What the Hub Offers the Restoration

The CEA Agritech Hub is a year-round, indoor, climate-controlled complement to an outdoor river restoration whose public activation will be seasonal. Five concrete alignments:

| # | Hub asset | Restoration alignment | Lead persona |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **"River → Plate" K-12 unit** — 6-week NGSS-aligned UbD curriculum (MS-LS2-1, MS-LS2-3, MS-ESS3-3, MS-ETS1-1) with 25 daily lesson plans | Brings Kent ISD classrooms into the river's recovery story from a controlled environment students can visit in February | Prof. Elena Cruz |
| 2 | **Year-round agri-tourism hub** — guided tours, workshops, farm-to-table dinners, private events; 10-comp validated pricing ([IALR](https://ialr.org/), [Purdue Overbeck](https://ag.purdue.edu/department/hla/agriculture-innovation/overbeck-farm/), [Downtown Market GR](https://downtownmarketgr.com/), [Meijer Gardens](https://www.meijergardens.org/), [JBZ](https://www.jbzoo.org/), [GRCM](https://www.grcm.org/)) | Captures winter + shoulder-season visitation that an outdoor whitewater experience cannot; feeds guests into GR Whitewater in warm months | Jordan Rivers |
| 3 | **Pollinator education apiary** — Michigan-native pollinator species map + [MSU Pollinator Performance Center](https://pollinators.msu.edu/) partnership; visitor-safe viewing hive | Supports the riparian-pollinator recovery story of the restoration with an on-site teaching asset | Amira Bloom |

## 5. What the Hub Commits

1. **No in-channel hydro installation** in the Lower Grand River restoration reach, in perpetuity, unless and until (a) restoration is complete, (b) a side-channel bypass concept passes an independent feasibility review, and (c) GR Whitewater, the City, EGLE, USACE, and USFWS all approve in writing.
2. **Public, version-controlled research transparency.** All Hub research, assumptions, and data flow through the [public GitHub repository](https://github.com/edwardslamont616-cell/Grand-Rapids-CEA-Agritech-Hub). The re-scoped energy model, the corrected load schedule ([12.18–14.7 GWh/yr](https://github.com/edwardslamont616-cell/Grand-Rapids-CEA-Agritech-Hub/blob/research/council-sprint-2026-04-23/research/02_lighting_loads/load_schedule_v01.md)), and the honest phased revenue ramp ([$2.2–2.7 M Y1, $9.1–16.0 M Y3](https://github.com/edwardslamont616-cell/Grand-Rapids-CEA-Agritech-Hub/blob/research/council-sprint-2026-04-23/research/07_integration/INTEGRATION_MEMO.md)) are already public.
3. **Interpretive partnership.** The Hub will host GR Whitewater and City signage, co-brand the River → Plate education program, and route visitor traffic to and from the river walk whenever operationally possible.
4. **Habitat-first operations.** Pesticide-free IPM ([biological-first standard of care](https://github.com/edwardslamont616-cell/Grand-Rapids-CEA-Agritech-Hub/blob/research/council-sprint-2026-04-23/research/03_crops/ipm_standard_of_care.md)), pollinator-positive landscaping, and drainage management consistent with the restoration's sediment and thermal goals.
