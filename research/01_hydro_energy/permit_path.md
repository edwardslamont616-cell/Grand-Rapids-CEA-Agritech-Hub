## OVERVIEW

This document maps every agency, stakeholder, and legal trigger relevant to a hydroelectric installation on the Grand River at the CEA Campus site. It overlays the regulatory timeline against the Lower Grand River Habitat Restoration Project construction schedule. Any hydro development strategy must navigate — or work cooperatively alongside — the restoration project's timeline.

## CRITICAL CONFLICTS AND FLAGS

### Flag 1: Snuffbox Mussel (Epioblasma triquetra)
The federally endangered snuffbox mussel is present in the Grand River project reach. In summer 2024, GRWW relocated **9,040 mussels** (including 38 federally endangered specimens) from the Lower Reach project area. Any new in-river construction activity would trigger mandatory Section 7 ESA consultation with USFWS, potentially requiring formal consultation (not merely informal), which can add 12–18 months. Jeopardy findings could trigger project modification or denial.

### Flag 2: NRCS PL-566 Fund Conditions
The $11.03M NRCS PL-566 grant carries conditions that the watershed improvement project achieve its stated conservation objectives. Installing a power-generating structure that alters the hydraulic regime or impedes the restored rapids would likely violate grant conditions and trigger federal clawback liability for the City of Grand Rapids. GRWW and the City would have strong legal incentive to oppose any conflicting installation.

### Flag 3: Upper Reach EIS Timeline
The USACE EIS for the 6th Street Dam removal and sea lamprey barrier replacement is running concurrently (Draft EIS expected Spring–Summer 2027; Final EIS Winter 2028). A hydroelectric project at or near the 6th Street Dam reach could be absorbed into this EIS as a "connected action," dramatically extending the FERC review process.

### Flag 4: FERC 10-MW Exemption vs. License
Per updated guidance ([National Hydropower Association, October 2025](https://hydro.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/Classroom-Presentations_Emily_Waters_SmallHydroLicensing_Wednesday_V3-Emily-Waters.pdf)), the 10-MW exemption at an existing dam or natural water feature still requires three-stage pre-filing consultation (18 CFR § 4.38) and is subject to mandatory fish/wildlife conditions. With a restoration EIS actively underway in the same reach, FERC may find that a comprehensive EIS — not an EA — is required, triggering the longer track.

## RECOMMENDED COORDINATION SEQUENCE

1. **Immediate:** Send coordination letter to Grand Rapids Whitewater (see [gr_whitewater_coordination_letter.md](../outbound_drafts/gr_whitewater_coordination_letter.md)) requesting meeting to discuss CEA Campus siting, restoration design, and potential future hydro integration.

2. **Q3 2026:** Request EGLE Part 301 pre-application meeting for a prospective side-channel bypass concept (not in-channel) — after restoration project is underway and design is confirmed. (See [egle_preapp_request.md](../outbound_drafts/egle_preapp_request.md))

3. **Q3 2026:** Submit Consumers Energy Level 3 DER interconnection pre-application for PV+BESS system. (See [consumers_energy_interconnection_inquiry.md](../outbound_drafts/consumers_energy_interconnection_inquiry.md))

4. **Q4 2026–Q1 2027:** Participate in USACE Upper Reach EIS public scoping as an interested party. Submit comments identifying the CEA Campus and requesting that any barrier/grade control structure designs consider side-channel hydro potential.

5. **2028 (post-Lower Reach completion):** Commission hydraulic engineering study of grade control structures to assess available head and flow for side-channel bypass. Use results to determine whether FERC pre-filing consultation is warranted.
