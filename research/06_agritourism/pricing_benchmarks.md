## Key Pricing Insights

1. **The $22–28 tour price point is well-supported** by the local market (Meijer Gardens at $22 general admission; smaller, intimate guided experiences nationally at $25–50). The Hub's active-growing environment and tasting component justify the premium over a passive garden walk.

2. **Michigan has no statutory agritourism liability protection** (as of the most recent PSU law survey). The Hub must rely on: (a) comprehensive general liability insurance, (b) signed participant waivers for beekeeping activities, (c) clearly posted risk notices. This is a meaningful cost line in the P&L — budget $15,000–25,000/year for commercial general liability + umbrella coverage. ([PSU Agritourism Laws](https://aglaw.psu.edu/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/20240726-ABDC-Agritourism-WEBSITE.pdf); [Michigan Farm Bureau](https://www.michfb.com/agriculture/political-action/policy-development/direct-marketing-and-agritourism))

3. **Dinner events at $95–145 are in the commercial sweet spot** for the GR market — above casual dining but accessible to the convention-delegate audience. Events at $150+ risk volume constraints given GR's mid-market demographics.

4. **Workshop pricing at $85–125** is 3–5x the Blandford benchmark, which is appropriate given: (a) downtown location premium, (b) live CEA system engagement vs. nature walk, (c) professional-grade instruction from Amira Bloom (Apiary Specialist) and Dr. Maya Green (Lead Horticulturist).

5. **Membership at $120/household** sits at the midpoint of the local benchmark range (Meijer $125; GRAM $100+) and should include tangible recurring value (monthly harvest share, workshop discount) to sustain renewal rates above 60%.
