# Grand Rapids Corridor Partners Map
**Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub — Partnership Ecosystem**
*Prepared by Jordan Rivers, Hospitality & Agri-Tourism Specialist | CEA Campus Design Council*

---

## Strategic Context

The Lower Grand River restoration (in-river construction beginning August 2025, with public access expected 2026+) is the catalytic event that activates the entire corridor. The 2.5-mile stretch will support paddleboarding, tubing, kayaking, canoeing, rafting, and angling — drawing a new cohort of active-leisure visitors to within walking distance of the Hub site. Grand Rapids already recorded **2.36 million hotel rooms sold in 2025** (+3.2% over 2024 record) and $290.6M in hotel revenue. The Hub enters a marketplace that is growing, not peaking.

## Partner Map

### Tier 1 — Anchor Partnerships (Must-Have, Year-One)

#### 1. Experience Grand Rapids (EXGR)
- **Role:** Official DMO for Grand Rapids/Kent County; leads leisure + group tourism marketing
- **Key Data:** 533 groups contracted in 2025 generating 173,738 room nights and 481,024 attendees; $193.5M estimated economic impact from group sales alone
- **Partnership Angle:** Register as an Official Attraction Partner → appear in EXGR's Visitor Guide, digital itinerary builder, and group planner resources. Request FAM tour for hotel concierges and group sales managers. Pitch a "Farm & River" package (Hub tour + Grand River paddle rental) as a signature GR experience for group itineraries.
- **Public Contact:** [www.experiencegr.com](https://www.experiencegr.com); partnerships listed at experiencegr.com/partner; VP of Marketing Kate Lieto publicly quoted (WGVU, March 2025)
- **Priority Action:** Submit Official Attraction listing application 6 months pre-open; request inclusion in 2026 group sales kit

#### 2. Downtown Grand Rapids Inc. (DGRI)
- **Role:** Downtown Development Authority; manages public realm, Downtown Ambassadors, business partnerships, World of Winter
- **Key Data:** World of Winter 2025 drew 2.3 million visitors (67% from outside region), $38M economic output, 335 jobs supported; Downtown Ambassadors assisted visitors 150,000+ times in 2022
- **Partnership Angle:** GR Forward initiative positions Grand River corridor as priority economic + ecological asset. The Hub is a physical manifestation of GR Forward goals. Request introductory meeting to discuss inclusion in "Downtown Discovery" Ambassador recommendation lists, World of Winter pop-up (a glowing greenhouse experience is a natural WoW anchor), and DDA investment tools for infrastructure.
- **Public Contact:** [downtowngr.org](https://downtowngr.org); info@downtowngr.org for archived reports; general inquiries at (616) 776-7500
- **Priority Action:** Schedule site-visit meeting with DGRI Executive Director 9 months pre-open

#### 3. Grand Rapids Whitewater (GRWW)
- **Role:** Nonprofit driving dam removal and Grand River ecological restoration; $15M+ in private investment; project triggers leisure/sport tourism corridor
- **Key Data:** In-river construction began August 2025; activities enabled include paddleboarding, tubing, kayaking, canoeing, rafting, angling; project designed for accessibility from diverse downtown neighborhoods
- **Partnership Angle:** Natural programming synergy — "Paddle + Farm" day packages, river-access drop-in for Hub guests, joint marketing to adventure-tourism audience. GRWW's messaging emphasizes "new business development opportunities" along the corridor — the Hub is precisely that.
- **Public Contact:** [grandrapidswhitewater.org](https://grandrapidswhitewater.org); contact form on site
- **Priority Action:** Request partnership conversation + map overlapping site boundaries to plan pedestrian/bike wayfinding

### Tier 2 — Distribution Partners (Hotels & Concierge Network)

The Hotel District in downtown Grand Rapids encompasses 10+ hotels with 2,000+ guest rooms, bounded by US-131 (west), Michigan Street (north), Division Avenue (east), and Fulton Street (south). The Hotel District – Business Association (merged 2021) provides a single point of coordination.

| Hotel | Brand | Key Contact Channel | Partnership Angle |
|-------|-------|--------------------|--------------------|
| **Amway Grand Plaza** | IHG (historic anchor) | Concierge desk + Events team | Iconic GR hotel; high convention group traffic; offer group package for convention delegates |
| **JW Marriott Grand Rapids** | Marriott Luxury | Concierge + Catering | Highest-tier leisure traveler; best candidate for premium farm-to-table dinner packages and corporate retreats |
| **Canopy by Hilton** | Hilton Lifestyle | Front desk lifestyle team | Younger, experience-seeking demographic; social-media-native guest; best candidate for social content and influencer packages |
| **Hyatt Place Grand Rapids/Downtown** | Hyatt | Concierge | Business and group traveler; offer corporate team-building day packages |
| **AC Hotel Grand Rapids** | Marriott | Front desk lifestyle team | Design-forward, millennial professional; natural fit for workshop and membership gifting |
| **Residence Inn Grand Rapids Downtown** | Marriott Extended Stay | Front desk | Extended-stay guests (consultants, relocators) are ideal membership candidates |

- **Priority Action:** Provide printed "Concierge Kit" (brochure + QR booking link + comp pair of tickets for each property) delivered 4 weeks before opening. Propose commission structure of 10–15% on booked experiences via concierge referral.

## FAM Tour Strategy (Familiarization Tours for Trade Channels)

A structured FAM tour program converts distribution partners into active evangelists. Recommended schedule:
1. **T-minus 8 weeks:** Hotel concierge FAM tour (30 minutes, guided, with tasting) — all 6 major downtown hotels
2. **T-minus 6 weeks:** EXGR group sales team FAM tour + press preview
3. **T-minus 4 weeks:** Media FAM + photo/video day (WZZM, FOX 17, MLive, Rapid Growth)
4. **T-minus 2 weeks:** Influencer/creator preview night
5. **Opening weekend:** Public grand opening with DGRI Ambassador promotion
