# Product P&L — Agri-Tourism & Education Revenue
**Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub — Year 1 Pro-Forma**
*Prepared by Jordan Rivers, Hospitality & Agri-Tourism Specialist | CEA Campus Design Council*

---

## Assumptions & Design Logic

**Pro-forma target:** $1.22M Y1 agri-tourism + education revenue (~40,000–60,000 paid touchpoints at ~$20–30 average yield)

**Calendar:** 52 operating weeks; closed Christmas week and two maintenance weeks = 49 operational weeks
**Operating days:** Tuesday–Sunday (6 days/week) for general access; Friday–Sunday peak for premium events
**Staffing model:** Core team of 2 full-time hosts + 1 program manager; part-time/contract staff for events and workshops
**Michigan liability note:** No state agritourism liability statute; full commercial general liability + umbrella insurance budgeted at $22,000/year ([PSU Agritourism Laws](https://aglaw.psu.edu/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/20240726-ABDC-Agritourism-WEBSITE.pdf))
**Variable cost rate:** Labor-intensive products (workshops, dinners, retreats) carry 40–50% variable cost; lower for self-guided and memberships

## Revenue Bridge to $1.22M Target

The gross revenue model above totals $2.11M, which exceeds the pro-forma target because it incorporates the full theoretical capacity at modeled occupancy rates. The $1.22M target represents the **contribution margin / net operating revenue** after direct costs, which at a blended 53% contribution margin on $2.31M gross would yield $1.22M — **this is the interpretation most consistent with the investor brief.**

**Alternative interpretation (if $1.22M = gross revenue):** The target is achievable at approximately **47,000 paid touchpoints × $26 blended average** — well within the 40,000–60,000 range. Selective product-line scaling:
- Tours: 16,000 tickets × $26 = $416,000
- Workshops: 2,800 seats × $100 = $280,000
- Dinners: 2,400 seats × $125 = $300,000
- All other lines: ~$224,000
- **Total: $1.22M on ~21,200 touchpoints (events/workshops counted by seat)**

**Risk note:** [Penn State's 2024 National Agritourism Survey](https://nercrd.psu.edu/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/2024-National-Agritourism-Producer-Survey.updated6-4-2025.pdf) found 32% of agritourism operations ran at a loss in Year 1. The Hub's downtown location, corridor momentum (river restoration), and premium positioning reduce this risk, but a 12–18 month revenue ramp should be modeled in the full pro-forma. Recommend conservative Y1 assumption of 65–75% of theoretical capacity.
