# Guest Journey v1 — The Signature 2-Hour Experience
**Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub**
*Prepared by Jordan Rivers, Hospitality & Agri-Tourism Specialist | CEA Campus Design Council*
*Framework: ROSES v2 (Role, Objective, Scenario, Expected Output, Specifications)*

---

## ROSES v2 Brief

| Element | Definition |
|---------|-----------|
| **Role** | Jordan Rivers, guiding a group of 15–20 guests through the Hub's signature public tour |
| **Objective** | Deliver an emotionally resonant, intellectually rich, and commercially productive 2-hour experience that converts first-time visitors into returning guests and/or members |
| **Scenario** | Mixed-adult group (leisure visitors, hotel guests, convention delegates, local residents) booked via online ticketing; Saturday afternoon, late spring |
| **Expected Output** | Full narrative arc with timing, sensory cues, host talking points, and revenue-generating moments woven naturally into the experience |
| **Specifications** | 2 hours, 15–20 guests, $25–28/person; ADA accessible throughout; beekeeping-safe protocols active; weather-independent (fully indoor) |

---

## Experience Design Principles

Three agritourism fundamentals anchor the journey ([NC State Extension](https://content.ces.ncsu.edu/agritourism-your-way)):
1. **Something to see** — Living crops, active hives, flowing water
2. **Something to do** — Hands-on tasting, harvest, tactile engagement
3. **Something to buy** — Harvest basket, honey jar, membership, workshop seat

---

## Arrival + Pre-Experience Choreography (T-minus 15 to 0 min)

**Setting:** Riverfront entry plaza. Grand River visible through glass or framing. Signage: "Where Grand Rapids Grows."

| Time | Action | Host/Operations Notes |
|------|--------|----------------------|
| T–15 | Digital check-in via QR kiosk or front desk | Staff greets by name if group booking; confirm any allergy/bee-sting disclosures (waiver pre-signed online) |
| T–10 | Welcome lounge opens: welcome beverage (Hub-grown herb-infused water or seasonal shrub) | Soft ambient sound — light hum of hydro system, birdsong. Merchandise and retail visible but not foregrounded. |
| T–5 | Host introduction in lounge | "Today you'll go inside the growing heart of Grand Rapids. You're going to taste things that were harvested 20 minutes ago. You're going to meet 100,000 bees. And you're going to leave understanding exactly where your food comes from." |
| T–0 | Group walk through threshold door | Threshold is intentionally designed: temperature shifts, humidity rises, green expands into view. The reveal is the emotional anchor. |

## Act 1 — The Living Lab (0:00–0:35)

**Space:** Hydroponic growing floors — leafy greens, herbs, microgreens

**Host narrative opening:**
> "Most people have never stood inside a food system before. This is one. Everything you see here is alive, growing, and will be on someone's plate within 48 hours."

| Minute | Experience Beat | Sensory / Engagement Cue |
|--------|----------------|--------------------------|
| 0:00–0:05 | Overview of CEA: why controlled environment, why Grand Rapids, why this river | Panoramic view of growing rows under spectrum lighting |
| 0:05–0:15 | Nutrient solution station: host demonstrates pH/EC measurement | Guests invited to dip their finger — "this is basically plant Gatorade" |
| 0:15–0:25 | Harvest demonstration: host harvests basil or microgreens with scissors | Pass the tray — guests smell and taste freshly cut herb |
| 0:25–0:35 | Vertical tower section: strawberries or cherry tomatoes (seasonal) | Guests pluck a ripe tomato directly from the tower. **This is the emotional peak of Act 1.** |

**Host bridge line:** "In a moment we'll meet the pollinators that make this possible. But first — a quick look at the power that runs this whole place."

---

## Act 2 — The Engine Room (0:35–0:55)

**Space:** Hydro-electric / mechanical systems display area (viewing gallery or transparent enclosure)

| Minute | Experience Beat | Engagement Cue |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| 0:35–0:45 | Brief overview of hydro-electric system: the Grand River powers the lights that grow the food | "The river outside that window is doing work inside this building right now." Interactive energy-flow display or live generation meter visible. |
| 0:45–0:55 | Processing and value-added preview: honey extraction equipment, fermentation vessels, packing area | See the full circle: from seedling to packaged product. Host names retail products available for purchase at close. |

**Transition:** "Ready to meet 100,000 neighbors?"

---

## Act 3 — The Apiary (0:55–1:25)

**Space:** Observation apiary — glass-enclosed observation hive (visible but protected) + outdoor rooftop hive access (seasonally activated, with full protective gear)

**Safety note:** All guests briefed in advance; bee-sting disclosure waiver signed during check-in. Staff trained per emergency protocol (see operations_sop_framework.md). Observation hive visits are glass-separated and require no protective gear.

| Minute | Experience Beat | Engagement Cue |
|--------|----------------|----------------|
| 0:55–1:05 | Observation hive introduction: find the queen, count cells, understand the waggle dance | "Somewhere in there is a queen who lays 1,500 eggs a day. Your job is to find her." |
| 1:05–1:15 | Honey tasting station: 3 varietal honeys (seasonal/location-based) + comb honey | Flavor comparison guided by host: "Wildflower vs. clover vs. buckwheat — same bees, different forage." |
| 1:15–1:25 | Pollination ecology: how the apiary feeds the greenhouse, how the greenhouse feeds the bees | "These two systems — the growing towers and the hives — are talking to each other every day." |

**Upsell moment (natural, not pushy):** "If this lit you up, our 3-hour Beekeeping Immersion workshop runs every other Saturday — I'll leave signup cards in the lounge."

---

## Act 4 — The Table (1:25–1:50)

**Space:** Harvest table / tasting room — communal seating, natural light, river view if possible

**Purpose:** This is the sensory culmination and the commercial conversion moment.

| Minute | Experience Beat | Notes |
|--------|----------------|-------|
| 1:25–1:35 | Seated tasting: Hub-grown composed small plate or harvest board (3–4 components: fresh greens, herb vinaigrette, honey-dressed item, one preserved/fermented element) | Menu changes weekly based on harvest. Printed card with today's ingredients. |
| 1:35–1:45 | Story of the plate: host or chef walks through each element — where it grew, who tended it, when it was harvested | "Every item on this board was alive in this building within the last 48 hours." |
| 1:45–1:50 | Membership pitch — warm, not transactional: "If you want to come back as often as you like, taste what's new each week, and get first access to dinners and workshops..." | Membership cards and brochures at each seat. Staff positioned to answer questions. |

## Revenue Moments Summary

| Moment | Product | Target Yield |
|--------|---------|-------------|
| Ticket purchase (pre-tour) | Guided tour | $25–28/person |
| During tour — workshop signup | Beekeeping / Hydroponics workshop | $85–125/seat |
| During tasting — membership offer | Annual household membership | $95–135 |
| Departure retail | Harvest pack, honey, merchandise | $8–15/person add-on |
| Group booking conversion | Private tour, corporate retreat | $150–350/head |

**Estimated per-visitor revenue (blended):** $32–45 when add-on capture is 35–50% of visitors

## Group Size & Capacity Notes

- Standard public tour: 15–20 guests, 1 host + 1 support staff
- Premium private tour: up to 25 guests, 1 host + 1 support
- School groups: up to 30 students, 2 Hub educators + teacher chaperones (separate school-program script)
- Minimum viable group for operational break-even: 8 guests at $25 = $200/tour gross ([UVM Extension Budgeting Guide](https://www.uvm.edu/d10-files/documents/2024-09/Module-3-Budgeting.pdf))

---

## Accessibility Notes
- Full ADA compliance throughout route (no steps, no gravel, minimum 36" pathways)
- Audio description available via app or staff for visually impaired guests
- Service animals welcome (see full ADA policy in accessibility_safety_plan.md)
- Sensory-friendly Saturday morning tours (reduced group size, quieter music, no surprise stimuli) available monthly
