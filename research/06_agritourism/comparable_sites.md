# Comparable Sites Research
**Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub — Agri-Tourism Benchmarking**
*Prepared by Jordan Rivers, Hospitality & Agri-Tourism Specialist | CEA Campus Design Council*

---

## Overview

This table benchmarks 10 comparable facilities across three lenses: (1) CEA/agri-education venues nationally, (2) Grand Rapids cultural attractions, and (3) regional nature/farm-education programs. The goal is to establish realistic visitor-volume and revenue anchors for the pro-forma projection of ~40,000–60,000 annual paid touchpoints at an average yield of $20–30.

## Cross-Site Synthesis

### Visitor Volume Calibration
The pro-forma target of 40,000–60,000 paid touchpoints is **ambitious but achievable** given the local market context:
- Blandford Nature Center is sold out months in advance, indicating supply-constrained demand for guided nature education in GR.
- GRCM and GRPM together serve hundreds of school groups annually at downtown locations.
- Meijer Gardens draws 785,000 visits annually in a suburban setting — the downtown riverfront location offers higher walk-in conversion than any suburban comp.
- The World of Winter festival alone drew 2.3 million visitors to downtown Grand Rapids in 2025, with 67% from outside the local region, confirming the corridor's leisure-tourism infrastructure.

### Pricing Positioning
| Product | Local Low Anchor | Hub Target | Premium Justification |
|---------|-----------------|-----------|----------------------|
| School field trip | $9 (Blandford) | $12–15/student | Indoor, year-round, STEM CEA curriculum |
| General admission / guided tour | $12–19 (GRAM/JBZ) | $22–28/adult | Small-group, expert-led, active growing environment |
| Workshop (adult) | $20–25 (Blandford) | $65–125 | Hands-on beekeeping / hydro / fermentation skills |
| Membership household | $85–125 (Meijer, GRAM) | $95–135 | Unlimited tours + harvest share + workshop discount |

### Programming Model Insights
- **Meijer Gardens' $4.46M in educational program revenue** (FY2024) with a $2.02M cost base implies ~54% margins on education — affirming the Hub's high-margin thesis for workshop and tour programming.
- **IALR's "Leave the Conference Room, Enter the Greenhouse"** format (conference content embedded in live growing space) is directly applicable to the Hub's corporate retreat and professional development product.
- **Blandford's over-subscription** validates the need for expanded capacity in GR's guided nature/science education market. The Hub fills this gap for urban/food-focused programming.
