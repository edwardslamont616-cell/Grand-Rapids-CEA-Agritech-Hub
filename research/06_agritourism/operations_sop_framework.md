# Operations SOP Framework — Run-of-Show Templates
**Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub**
*Prepared by Jordan Rivers, Hospitality & Agri-Tourism Specialist | CEA Campus Design Council*

---

## Framework Purpose

This document provides standardized run-of-show (ROS) templates for each primary product type. Each template defines pre-event, during-event, and post-event responsibilities by role. Templates are designed to be printed and carried by staff on duty.

**Staff Role Abbreviations:**
- **PH** = Primary Host (tour guide / workshop facilitator)
- **SS** = Support Staff (floor assistant / retail)
- **PM** = Program Manager (administrative oversight; present or on-call)
- **KIT** = Kitchen/Culinary staff (event-specific)
- **SEC** = Security/Safety designee

## Template 1: Guided Greenhouse Tour (Public, 90 min)

**Group size:** 12–20 | **Price:** $25–28/person | **Lead time required:** Online booking 24 hrs+ in advance

### Pre-Tour (T–60 to T–0 min)
| Time | Task | Owner |
|------|------|-------|
| T–60 | Confirm booking roster; note any allergy/bee-sting disclosures from online waiver | PM |
| T–45 | Walk the tour route: confirm all systems active, no wet floors, lighting functional | PH |
| T–30 | Prep tasting station: cut herbs, plate microgreens, portion honey, arrange taste cards | SS + KIT |
| T–20 | Retail area staged: harvest bundles, honey, merchandise, membership brochures | SS |
| T–15 | Welcome lounge ready: welcome beverages poured, ambient sound active | SS |
| T–10 | Host positioned at entry; QR check-in kiosk or manual check-in list ready | PH + SS |
| T–0 | Group assembled in welcome lounge; count heads against reservation | PH |

### During Tour (0–90 min)
| Time | Beat | Owner | Notes |
|------|------|-------|-------|
| 0:00–0:05 | Welcome remarks + safety briefing (emergency exits, bee proximity rules, no touching crops without host direction) | PH | Read from approved script; do not skip safety briefing |
| 0:05–0:35 | Act 1: Living Lab (hydro system, harvest demo, tasting) | PH | SS floats to assist mobility needs or manage straggler guests |
| 0:35–0:55 | Act 2: Engine Room + processing area | PH | Maintain group cohesion; max 2-minute hold at any station |
| 0:55–1:25 | Act 3: Apiary | PH (+ Amira Bloom or qualified apiary host) | **Two-staff minimum when near hive area**; bee-sting protocol card in host pocket |
| 1:25–1:50 | Act 4: Harvest Table tasting | PH + SS at table | Membership pitch at 1:45; leave materials at each seat |
| 1:50–2:00 | Closing remarks + retail time | PH signals; SS positioned at retail | PH thanks group; invites social sharing (@HubGrandRapids) |

### Post-Tour (T+0 to T+30 min)
| Task | Owner |
|------|-------|
| Reset tasting station for next tour | SS |
| Record headcount and any issues in tour log | PH |
| Flag any incidents (allergic reactions, guest complaints, equipment issues) to PM | PH |
| Collect membership signups and enter in CRM | SS |
| Post-tour automated email sent to all guests (via booking platform) | PM / automated |

### Emergency Protocols during Tour
- **Bee sting:** Immediately ask guest if they have known allergy. If unknown reaction developing, administer Hub EpiPen (trained staff only); call 911; move group to safe zone. See full bee-sting protocol in accessibility_safety_plan.md.
- **Allergic reaction (food):** Do not administer food allergens; if guest presents anaphylaxis, call 911; administer EpiPen if trained and available.
- **Guest fall/injury:** Do not move guest unless in immediate danger; call 911; complete incident report within 1 hour.
- **Severe weather:** Move group to designated interior shelter zone (main event room, away from glass); do not dismiss until all-clear.

## Template 2: Adult Workshop (2–3 hr)

**Group size:** 12–20 | **Price:** $85–125 | **Lead time:** Online booking 72+ hrs in advance

### Pre-Workshop (T–90 to T–0 min)
| Task | Owner |
|------|-------|
| Confirm roster; print name tents | PM |
| Confirm dietary restrictions if workshop includes food components | PM |
| Station setup: tools, materials, workbench positions (1 per guest), safety gloves/gear staged | PH + SS |
| Ingredients/live materials (bees, seedlings, ferment cultures) prepared and safe | PH + expert (Amira or Dr. Maya Green as appropriate) |
| Waiver compliance confirmed for beekeeping workshops | PM |

### During Workshop (0–180 min)
| Segment | Description | Owner |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| 0–15 min | Welcome + introduction; safety overview; workshop agenda | PH |
| 15–90 min | Core hands-on instruction: skill demonstration + guest practice | PH + expert |
| 90–120 min | Independent practice / guided work | PH + SS circulate |
| 120–150 min | Results/output review: tasting, show-and-tell, Q&A | PH |
| 150–165 min | Wrap-up: take-home materials packaged, next steps shared | PH + SS |
| 165–180 min | Upsell transition: membership, next-level workshop, upcoming events | PH |

### Post-Workshop
| Task | Owner |
|------|-------|
| Station cleaning and reset | SS |
| Waste disposal per MDARD food-handling guidelines | KIT/SS |
| Workshop evaluation form collected (paper or QR digital) | SS |
| Any live material (bees, cultures) returned to safe environment | Amira/Dr. Green |

## Template 3: Farm-to-Table Dinner (3–4 hr)

**Group size:** 30–60 | **Price:** $95–145/seat | **Lead time:** Pre-sold 3+ weeks; venue set 4 hrs before event

### Pre-Event (T–4 hrs to T–0)
| Time | Task | Owner |
|------|------|-------|
| T–4 hrs | Final menu confirmed with kitchen; dietary restriction accommodations flagged | PM + KIT |
| T–3 hrs | Table layout set; linen, centerpieces (Hub-grown herbs/flowers), printed menu cards | SS |
| T–2 hrs | Chef walk-through of evening flow; timing per course confirmed | PM + KIT |
| T–2 hrs | Bar/beverage station staged; Michigan craft beer/wine pre-approved per MDARD license | SS/KIT |
| T–1 hr | Lighting and ambient sound dialed in | SS/PM |
| T–30 min | Host/greeter positioned at entry; guest list printed | PH/SS |
| T–15 min | Welcome drink poured at each seat or at entry station | SS |

### Evening Flow
| Time | Beat | Owner |
|------|------|-------|
| 0:00–0:30 | Cocktail hour / greenhouse viewing; optional mini-tour (10 min, self-guided with signage) | PH circulates; SS manages bar |
| 0:30–0:45 | Guests seated; host welcome remarks + story of tonight's menu | PH |
| 0:45–1:15 | Course 1 + Course 2; host or chef describes each dish | KIT + SS service |
| 1:15–1:45 | Course 3 (main); brief "Meet the Bees" moment — Amira introduces honey-based component | KIT + Amira |
| 1:45–2:15 | Course 4 (cheese/dessert); membership and upcoming event announcement | PH |
| 2:15–2:30 | Dessert + final wine/cocktail; informal conversation; music | All |
| 2:30–3:00 | Departure; guests receive small take-home gift (honey jar or herb bundle) | SS at door |

### Post-Event
| Task | Owner |
|------|-------|
| Kitchen breakdown and cleaning per MDARD temporary food service standards | KIT |
| Incident log completed | PM |
| Guest emails sent next morning: thank-you + next dinner booking link | PM / automated |
| Revenue reconciliation completed by next business day | PM |

### MDARD Licensing Notes
Michigan Food Law (Act 92, PA 2000) requires a food service license for all food prepared and served to the public. For recurring dinner events at a fixed location, a **permanent food service license** (issued by Kent County Health Department) is required — not just a temporary license. Temporary licenses are valid up to 14 consecutive days and require application 5 business days prior ([CMDHD Michigan Food Law](https://www.cmdhd.org/tempfoodevent)). Begin permanent licensing process 90 days before opening.

## Template 5: School Field Trip (K–12, 2 hr)

**Group size:** 20–30 students + 4–6 adult chaperones | **Price:** $12–15/student; chaperones complimentary (1:5 ratio required)
**Booking:** Minimum 14 days in advance; educational objectives form required

### Preparation
- Confirm grade level and learning objectives (aligned to NGSS or Michigan Social Studies Standards)
- Print pre-visit teacher packet (mailed or emailed 1 week before)
- Two Hub educators assigned; one educator per 15 students
- Confirm allergy/medical forms received

### Day-of Flow
| Time | Beat | Educator Notes |
|------|------|----------------|
| 0:00–0:15 | Welcome + norms setting: "Scientists observe. We ask why." | Engage curiosity; set expectations |
| 0:15–0:40 | Hydro system walkthrough: plant biology, light, water, nutrients | Hands-on: pH strips, plant smell/touch |
| 0:40–1:00 | Apiary viewing (glass observation only for groups under 13; full hive demo for 13+) | Amira or trained educator leads |
| 1:00–1:20 | Harvest + tasting station: students harvest microgreens, taste | Allergen-safe; no known allergens used |
| 1:20–1:45 | Classroom wrap-up activity: "Design Your Own Growing System" sketch (worksheet included) | Educational takeaway |
| 1:45–2:00 | Q&A + gift: seed packet to take home | Reinforces connection to growing |

## Template 6: Self-Guided Experience (Weekday, 60–90 min)

**Group size:** 1–10 | **Price:** $13 | **No advance booking required** (walk-in or same-day)

- Guest scans QR at entry for digital audio guide (10 numbered stops, each 3–5 min)
- Staff present but not dedicated — check-in at front desk, available for questions
- Tasting not included (add-on available at Harvest Bar: $6)
- Retail shop open throughout
- Accessibility: audio guide available in English and Spanish; large-print alternative available
