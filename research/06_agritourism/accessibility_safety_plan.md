# Accessibility & Safety Plan
**Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub**
*Prepared by Jordan Rivers, Hospitality & Agri-Tourism Specialist | CEA Campus Design Council*

## 1. ADA Accessibility

### Legal Foundation
The Americans with Disabilities Act requires equal access for all individuals with disabilities at any facility open to the public. **No public-facing event or experience is exempt from ADA requirements** — this applies to all agri-tourism operations, including CEA facilities ([AgMRC ADA Agritourism Guide](https://www.agmrc.org/foodsystems/agritourism/rules-and-regulations)). Michigan's Persons with Disabilities Civil Rights Act (MCL §37.1101 et seq.) provides parallel state-level protections.

### Physical Access
| Element | Standard | Hub Implementation |
|---------|---------|-------------------|
| Pathways | Min. 36" clear width (ADA); 44" preferred | All tour routes minimum 48" clear |
| Flooring | Non-slip, stable, firm surface | Sealed concrete or textured tile throughout; no loose gravel |
| Thresholds | Max. ½" raised threshold | No raised thresholds; all doors are push-button or pull with lever handle |
| Doorways | Min. 32" clear (36" recommended) | All doorways min. 36" |
| Restrooms | Full ADA-compliant restrooms required | Minimum 1 unisex ADA-compliant restroom with grab bars, turning radius, accessible fixtures |
| Signage | High-contrast, large print, Braille on room ID signs | Implement at all stations, exits, and restroom doors |
| Parking | 1 van-accessible space per 6 accessible spaces required | Coordinate with building/lot owner for minimum 2 accessible spaces (1 van-accessible) |
| Ramps / elevators | Required where stairs exist | No stairs in tour route; elevator required if multi-level facility |

### Apiary Access Note
The apiary observation zone (glass-enclosed) must be accessible to wheelchair users. Outdoor rooftop hive visits are optional add-ons; if the rooftop is not accessible, equivalent beekeeping engagement must be available at the ground-level observation hive at no additional cost or penalty to guests with disabilities. ([ADA agritourism guidance — YouTube/Extension, 2025](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-e0FII-08tk))

### Sensory and Communication Access
- **Audio description:** Digital tour guide (QR-activated) available with audio narration for visually impaired guests; large-print alternative available on request
- **Sign language interpretation:** Available with 7-day advance request; contact Hub program manager
- **Sensory-friendly tours:** Offered monthly — reduced group size (max 10), quieter ambient sound, no sudden sensory changes, extra time at each station
- **Language access:** Digital guide available in English and Spanish at launch; additional languages added based on demand

### Service Animals
Service animals (trained to perform a task for a person with a disability) must be permitted in all public areas, including the greenhouse and apiary viewing areas. Staff must not ask about the nature of the guest's disability. Staff may ask: (1) Is this a service animal required because of a disability? (2) What work or task has the dog been trained to perform? Emotional support animals are not covered under ADA Title III and may be declined. Coordinate with apiary specialist if a service animal accompanies a guest near the apiary: assess individual situation; do not automatically deny access. ([ADA service animal rules](https://www.ada.gov/topics/service-animals/))

### Digital Accessibility
The Hub website must comply with WCAG 2.1 Level AA:
- Alt text on all images
- Keyboard navigation enabled
- Sufficient color contrast ratios
- Captions on all video content
- Accessible booking/ticketing platform

## 2. Michigan Food Service Licensing

### Permanent Food Service License (Required for Recurring Events)
Michigan Food Law, Act 92, PA 2000, requires a food service license for any facility where food is prepared and served to the public. For the Hub's recurring farm-to-table dinners, workshops with food components, and harvest tasting stations, a **permanent food service license** is required. Licensing is administered by the Kent County Health Department (KCHD) Environmental Health Division.

**Process:**
1. Submit facility plan review application to KCHD ≥ 90 days before opening
2. Schedule pre-construction plan review meeting
3. Complete construction; request pre-opening inspection
4. Obtain license prior to first food service event

**Temporary License (for pre-opening events only):** Valid up to 14 consecutive days at one location; application submitted ≥ 5 business days before event; fee varies by organization type ([DHD4 Temporary Food License](https://www.dhd4.org/temporary-food-establishments); [CMDHD Michigan Food Law](https://www.cmdhd.org/tempfoodevent)).

### Food Safety Operations
- All food-handling staff must hold current Michigan Food Handler certification
- HACCP plan required for catered dinner events
- Proper allergen labeling required on all menu items
- Cold chain maintained for all perishable items; temperature logs kept
- Harvest-to-table traceability documentation maintained per FSMA requirements

### Allergen Management
Top 9 allergens (milk, eggs, fish, shellfish, tree nuts, peanuts, wheat, soybeans, sesame) must be disclosed on all menus. At booking, guests are prompted to disclose dietary restrictions. For school field trips and events, all allergen information is reviewed by the program manager before food service. Hub tasting stations default to allergen-aware preparation: no peanuts, no tree nuts, no shellfish. Gluten-free and dairy-free options available for all tasting components.

## 3. Bee-Sting Emergency Protocol

### Risk Context
The Hub's apiary program (100,000+ bees) creates a specific liability exposure for anaphylaxis. Approximately 1–7.5% of adults have systemic allergic reactions to bee stings; anaphylaxis risk is highest in previously stung individuals. Michigan has **no agritourism liability statute** offering protection for bee-related incidents ([PSU Agritourism Laws](https://aglaw.psu.edu/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/20240726-ABDC-Agritourism-WEBSITE.pdf)). Strict protocol is essential.

### Pre-Visit Disclosure
- All guests booking any experience that includes proximity to the apiary (tours, workshops, private events) must complete an allergy/sting disclosure form at booking and again at check-in
- Guests disclosing bee venom allergy are offered: (a) glass-observation-only apiary viewing, (b) a complete bee-sting protocol brief, (c) option to carry their own EpiPen
- Waiver language must be reviewed by Hub's liability attorney; included in all ticket purchase flows

### On-Site Preparedness
| Item | Location | Quantity |
|------|----------|---------|
| EpiPen (epinephrine auto-injector) | Welcome desk, apiary area, event room | 2 per location (standard + Jr.) |
| First aid kit | All above locations + kitchen | 1 per location |
| Emergency contact poster (911, KCHD, nearest ER: Spectrum Health Butterworth) | Each room + staff area | Posted prominently |
| Trained staff (EpiPen-certified) | Minimum 1 per shift | Certify all full-time staff; train all part-time |
| AED (automated external defibrillator) | Entry lobby and event room | 2 total |

### Response Protocol (Bee Sting)
1. **Confirm sting:** Remove stinger by scraping (not squeezing) with flat object; do not squeeze stinger (releases more venom)
2. **Assess guest:** Ask about known allergy; look for signs of anaphylaxis (hives, throat tightening, difficulty breathing, dizziness, drop in blood pressure)
3. **If anaphylaxis symptoms present:** Administer EpiPen (outer thigh, through clothing if necessary; hold 10 seconds); **call 911 immediately**; notify emergency services that epinephrine has been administered
4. **If no anaphylaxis symptoms:** Clean sting site; apply ice; monitor guest for 30 minutes; offer to contact emergency services
5. **Monitor for biphasic reaction:** Secondary anaphylaxis can occur up to 72 hours after initial exposure; advise guest to seek medical care regardless of initial recovery
6. **Documentation:** Complete incident report form within 1 hour; notify insurance carrier within 24 hours; retain signed waiver copy
7. **If fatality or serious injury:** Do not move victim unless necessary; preserve scene; contact Hub legal counsel and insurance carrier immediately

**Protocol source:** [Anaphylaxis and EpiPen Standard Protocol](https://www.smsacademy.org/wp-content/uploads/2025/04/Anaphylaxis-and-Epi-Pen%C2%AE-Standard-Protocol-1.pdf); [Healthline EpiPen Administration Guide](https://www.healthline.com/health/anaphylaxis/how-to-use-epipen)

## 4. Food Allergen Emergency Protocol

1. Guest reports allergic reaction to food consumed on premises
2. Staff immediately removes any remaining suspect food from guest's reach
3. Ask guest: severity of reaction; presence of own EpiPen; prior history
4. If throat closing, difficulty breathing, or systemic symptoms: **call 911; administer EpiPen if trained**
5. If mild (hives, stomach upset only): offer water; monitor; offer option to call EMS
6. Document all information; preserve food sample if possible for investigation
7. Complete incident report within 1 hour; notify insurance carrier

## 5. Severe Weather Plan

Grand Rapids is subject to severe thunderstorms, high winds, and tornado watches/warnings. The Hub's riverfront location has open exposure on at least one side.

### Warning Levels
| Level | Trigger | Action |
|-------|---------|--------|
| Watch | Conditions favorable for severe weather | Notify guests proactively; identify shelter zone; move outdoor-adjacent activities inside |
| Warning | Severe storm or tornado imminent | Suspend all outdoor access; move entire group to interior shelter zone immediately |
| Emergency | Tornado on ground or extreme wind | Interior shelter zone; away from windows, exterior walls, apiary rooftop; crouch, cover |

### Shelter Zone
- Designated interior shelter zone: main event room or interior hallway, minimum 2 rooms away from exterior glass
- All staff trained in shelter location and guest communication protocol
- Emergency announcement system (PA or staff verbal) tested monthly
- Outdoor programming (rooftop apiary visits) automatically suspended when weather radar indicates severe activity within 20 miles

## 7. Insurance Requirements

Given Michigan's absence of an agritourism liability statute, the Hub must carry:
- **Commercial General Liability (CGL):** Minimum $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate; higher limits recommended given bee/food risk ($2M/$4M)
- **Product Liability:** Covers food products sold on premises; included in CGL or as rider
- **Umbrella/Excess Liability:** $2–5M umbrella above CGL
- **Workers' Compensation:** Required by Michigan law (MCL §418.101 et seq.) for all employees
- **Liquor Liability:** Required if beer/wine service is included in events; separate rider or endorsement
- **Estimated annual premium:** $22,000–35,000 (CGL + umbrella + workers' comp)
