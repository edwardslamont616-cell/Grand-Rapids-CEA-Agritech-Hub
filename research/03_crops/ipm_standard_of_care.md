## Philosophy

The Grand Rapids CEA Hub operates a **biological-first IPM program**: preventive biocontrols are deployed proactively before pest pressure is observed. Chemical intervention is reserved as a last resort and must be compatible with all resident beneficial insects, particularly bumblebee pollinators in Zone C (tomato).

This approach is:
1. **Required for bumblebee compatibility** — most synthetic insecticides kill *Bombus impatiens* colonies
2. **Required for premium market positioning** — Meijer, Whole Foods, and hospital buyers increasingly require pesticide-free documentation
3. **Economically sound** — biocontrol programs cost $0.80–$2.50/m²/yr; reactive chemical programs cost more and risk crop loss

## Zone-by-Zone IPM Protocol

### Zone A — Microgreens (Highest Risk: Fungus Gnats, Damping-Off)

| Practice | Frequency | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| *S. feltiae* drench | Every 14 days to germination trays | Apply to moist substrate only; water temperature ≤60°F; most effective at 59–79°F substrate temperature ([KSU *S. feltiae* Guide](https://bookstore.ksre.ksu.edu/download/steinernema-feltiae-biological-control-agent-of-fungus-gnat-larvae_MF3649)) |
| Yellow sticky traps | Permanent (replace every 2 weeks) | Count adults per trap; action threshold = >1 adult/trap/week triggers S. feltiae increase |
| RootShield PLUS+ | Media incorporation or initial drench | Prevents *Pythium* damping-off; critical for basil and cilantro microgreens |
| Sanitation | Between every crop cycle | Remove all spent substrate; clean and UV-sterilize trays if pathogen pressure |
| Humidity management | Keep below 70% RH | Fungus gnat larvae require moist medium; reducing irrigation volume controls populations |

### Zone B — Leafy Greens and Herbs (Multi-Pest, Moderate-Complexity)

| Practice | Frequency | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| *A. swirskii* sachets | Every 4 weeks preventively | 1 sachet per 5–10 m² of canopy; hang in sheltered location, not direct light; effective 18–32°C ([Koppert Swirskii page](https://www.koppert.com/crop-protection/biological-pest-control/predatory-mites/amblyseius-swirskii/)) |
| *N. californicus* (Spical Ulti-Mite) | Introduce when temps <18°C | Swirskii loses efficacy below 18°C; californicus tolerates cooler conditions |
| *S. feltiae* drench | Monthly | For fungus gnats if NFT channels have organic matter buildup |
| BotaniGard ES spray | At first aphid or thrips sighting | Evening application; 4-hour REI; 0-day PHI ([BioWorks Product Page](https://bioworksinc.com/products/)) |
| Blue sticky traps | Permanent | Thrips prefer blue; count weekly |
| Yellow sticky traps | Permanent | Whitefly, fungus gnats; count weekly |
| Basil note | — | *A. swirskii* does NOT work on tomato (glandular hairs inhibit); in Zone B basil it performs excellently ([Koppert](https://www.koppert.com/crop-protection/biological-pest-control/predatory-mites/amblyseius-swirskii/)) |

### Zone C — Greenhouse Tomato (Critical: Bumblebee Compatibility Required)

> **CRITICAL:** All pesticide and biocontrol applications in Zone C must be compatible with *Bombus impatiens* colonies. Synthetic pyrethroid and neonicotinoid insecticides are **prohibited**. Beauveria and most biocontrols are compatible; always verify with Koppert/Biobest before any new product introduction.

| Practice | Frequency | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| *A. swirskii* preventive | Every 4 weeks from transplant | Sachets on plant; swirskii established before thrips/whitefly appears |
| *P. persimilis* | At first spider mite detection | Very fast-acting; do not use with sulfur fungicide (incompatible) |
| *O. insidiosus* (Thripor-L) | At first thrips detection or at flowering | Thrips are worst at flowering; Orius is effective predator of all stages |
| Bumblebee (*B. impatiens*) | 7–15 colonies/hectare; replace colony every 8–12 weeks | Standard of care per [OSU Bumblebee Pollination 2023](https://ohioline.osu.edu/factsheet/ent-0092); Koppert Natupol or Biobest Tripol |
| Yellow sticky traps (hung vertically) | Weekly count | Whitefly monitoring; action threshold 5/trap/week triggers *A. swirskii* increase |
| Anther bruising score | 3× per week at peak bloom | Score 1–5 per OSU protocol; target level 2–3; above 3 = over-pollination risk ([OSU](https://ohioline.osu.edu/factsheet/ent-0092)) |

## Environmental Setpoints for Biocontrol Efficacy

| Parameter | Optimal Range | Notes |
|-----------|--------------|-------|
| Temperature (Swirskii) | 20–32°C | Not effective below 18°C; above 35°C reduces efficacy |
| Relative Humidity (Swirskii) | >60% RH | Sensitive below 60%; sachets mitigate low-humidity impact |
| Temperature (S. feltiae) | 15–26°C substrate | Most effective in moist, warm growing media ([KSU](https://bookstore.ksre.ksu.edu/download/steinernema-feltiae-biological-control-agent-of-fungus-gnat-larvae_MF3649)) |
| Light (Beauveria spray) | Apply in low-light/evening | UV degrades spores; nighttime or overcast application improves efficacy |
| Bumblebee temperature | 18–28°C | Colonies perform best within normal greenhouse tomato range |

## Integration with Apiary (Amira Bloom — Pollination Ecologist)

Zone C greenhouse tomato uses **commercial *B. impatiens* colonies** (not campus honey bee hives). The campus *Apis mellifera* apiary:
- **Is not used for greenhouse tomato pollination** (honey bees are inferior to bumblebees for buzz pollination)
- **May provide supplemental pollination** for Zone B herbs and educational tours
- **All IPM applications in Zone B must also be honey bee-compatible** when apiary colonies are used near the herb greenhouse

Coordination protocol: Dr. Green and Amira Bloom to jointly approve any new biocontrol or chemical product before introduction to Zones B or C.

## Escalation Protocol (If Biocontrols Are Insufficient)

| Tier | Condition | Action | Products Allowed |
|------|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| 1 | Pest detected; below threshold | Increase biocontrol release rate | All biologicals |
| 2 | Threshold exceeded (5+ whitefly/trap/week; visible spider mite colonies) | Add secondary biocontrol agent; consult Koppert advisor | Orius, Persimilis, increase Swirskii |
| 3 | Outbreak threatening crop loss | Targeted soft chemistry (OMRI-listed or bee-safe) | Insecticidal soap (M-Pede), neem oil (spinosad with caution), kaolin clay — NEVER pyrethroids/neonics in Zone C |
| 4 | Zone-threatening | Remove affected plants; quarantine; reassess program | Full zone reset |
