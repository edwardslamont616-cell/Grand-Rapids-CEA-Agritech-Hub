## 1. Core Reframe

**The problem:** The current pro forma classifies soy processing alongside CEA production — treating tofu, soymilk, and soy oil as if they were grown in the same operational and regulatory context as lettuce, microgreens, and herbs. This conflation distorts both the revenue model and the regulatory pathway.

**The correction:** Soy processing is **food manufacturing**, not CEA production. It operates under:
- FDA 21 CFR Part 117 (Preventive Controls for Human Food), not the FSMA Produce Safety Rule
- SQF Food Manufacturing code, not Primary Plant Production
- A distinct physical space (Zone E) requiring dedicated allergen controls
- A separate capital investment plan, hiring plan, and revenue model

Separating the two tracks is not a demotion of soy's importance — it is the commercially and legally correct framing that will allow both businesses to succeed on their own terms.

## 4. USDA VAPG Fit

The USDA Value Added Producer Grant (VAPG) is the most directly applicable federal grant for this enterprise.

| Parameter | Details | Source |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| Grant type | Working Capital Grants (up to $200,000) or Planning Grants (up to $50,000) | [USDA Rural Development VAPG](https://www.rd.usda.gov/programs-services/business-programs/value-added-producer-grants) |
| Total available funding | ~$25 million nationally (FY2026) | [USDA RD VAPG](https://www.rd.usda.gov/programs-services/business-programs/value-added-producer-grants) |
| Match requirement | 1:1 match required (cash or in-kind) | USDA VAPG |
| Eligibility | Agricultural producers who own + produce >50% of the raw commodity being processed | USDA VAPG |
| Application deadline | April 22, 2026 (FY2026 cycle) | [simpler.grants.gov VAPG listing](https://simpler.grants.gov/opportunity/ba15a050-eb0d-4a1b-a0c5-5c8f345302ff) |
| Does soy processing qualify? | **Yes** — processing soybeans (a commodity the applicant produces or controls) into tofu/soymilk is a textbook value-added activity. USDA defines value-added as: physical change to the commodity creates a new product, or through segregation (identity-preserved non-GMO soy) | USDA VAPG program description |
| Priority points available | +5 pts for first-time applicants; + points for beginning/veteran/socially disadvantaged farmers; + points for <$125K request | USDA VAPG |

**Recommendation:** Submit a **Planning Grant ($50,000)** in the April 2026 cycle to fund the feasibility study, equipment specification, and food safety plan development for Zone E. This requires:
1. Producer documentation showing soybean production or contract sourcing
2. Preliminary business plan
3. Evidence of market demand (letters of intent from buyers)
4. 1:1 match ($50,000 in cash or documented in-kind)

## 7. Conclusion

Soy processing is a **high-margin, regionally differentiated, grant-eligible** opportunity that belongs in the Grand Rapids Hub — but as a **Phase 2 food processing enterprise**, not as a Year 1 CEA revenue line. The reframing:

- **Removes it from the CEA pro forma** (it is not CEA production)
- **Preserves the business case** (strong margins, VAPG grant eligible, Michigan soybean supply chain)
- **Clarifies the regulatory path** (FDA Part 117, not FSMA Produce Safety Rule)
- **Sequences capital investment appropriately** (Zone E CAPEX in Y2, not Y1)
