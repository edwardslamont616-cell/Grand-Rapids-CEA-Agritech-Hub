# Michigan/Midwest Buyer Target Map
**Dr. Maya Green, Lead Horticulturist & Crop Scientist**
*Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub — CEA Campus Design Council*
*Frameworks applied: CARE + Co-Star | Last updated: 2025*

---

## Overview

Grand Rapids is at the center of one of the strongest regional food procurement ecosystems in the Midwest. Gordon Food Service (HQ: Wyoming, MI) is 8 miles from most proposed Hub sites. SpartanNash (HQ: Byron Center, MI) covers 50+ stores. Corewell Health (HQ: Grand Rapids) has an active local purchasing commitment and already buys from local CEA farms.

This map covers 10 target buyer segments, with category buyer role guidance, product specs, volume requirements (where publicly available), and approach strategy.

---

## Buyer Target Map

### 1. Gordon Food Service (GFS)
**Type:** Broadline distributor / Retail stores
**HQ:** Wyoming, Michigan (immediately adjacent to Grand Rapids)
**Relationship signal:** GFS co-located a Square Roots indoor farm at their Wyoming HQ in 2021; sells local CEA herbs/microgreens through GFS Stores and restaurant distribution ([GFS, 2021](https://gfs.com/en-us/ideas/square-roots-and-gordon-food-service-celebrate-new-michigan-indoor-farm-expanding-partnership-to-grow-more-local-food-across-the-u-s/))

| Category | Contact/Channel | Typical Spec | Volume Expectations | Approach |
|----------|----------------|--------------|--------------------|---------| 
| Fresh herbs | Category Manager, Fresh Produce | Clamshell or sleeve, 0.5–1 oz packs, 14-day shelf life target, GAP certified | 500–2,000 units/week per SKU | Lead with local story + existing Square Roots precedent |
| Microgreens | Fresh Produce buyer | 2 oz retail clamshell or bulk 5 lb foodservice; shelf life 7–10 days | 200–1,000 units/week | Foodservice angle (restaurants via GFS distribution) |
| Leafy greens | Fresh Produce Category Mgr | 4–5 oz clamshell, case of 12 | 1,000–5,000 units/week at scale | Phase 2–3 volume; start with specialty items |
| Greenhouse tomato | Produce buyer | 1-lb cluster, USDA Grade 1, GAP/SQF cert | 500–2,000 lbs/week | Year 2 onward after production ramp |

**Key requirements:** USDA GAP minimum; SQF preferred at volume; allergen-free documentation; traceability to lot level.

**Approach strategy:**
1. Request intro meeting through Square Roots contact network (direct GFS precedent exists)
2. Bring 1-page product spec sheet + 3 sample SKUs
3. Propose a pilot purchase order (1–2 SKUs, 6-week run) before longer-term agreement
4. Emphasize 15-mile farm-to-warehouse advantage

### 2. SpartanNash
**Type:** Regional grocery retailer/distributor
**HQ:** Byron Center, Michigan
**Coverage:** ~50+ corporate retail stores (D&W Fresh Market, Family Fare, VG's Grocery, Forest Hills Foods) in Michigan
**CEA precedent:** Active buyer from Square Roots Michigan operations ([Blue Book Services, 2021](https://www.bluebookservices.com/square-roots-to-sell-local-produce-in-spartannash-stores-in-michigan/))

| Category | Spec | Volume | Approach |
|----------|------|--------|----------|
| Herbs (basil, cilantro) | Sleeve or clamshell, local branding required, GAP cert | 300–1,500 units/week across 50 stores | Leverage Square Roots CEA precedent; offer private-label option |
| Microgreens | 2–3 oz clamshell | 200–800 units/week | Restaurant-grade varieties for specialty section |
| Leafy greens | 4–5 oz clamshell | 2,000–8,000 units/week at scale | Long-term goal Phase 2 |

**Requirements:** Michigan-grown labeling preferred; USDA GAP; EDI ordering capability at scale.

**Approach strategy:**
- Contact: Director of Produce Merchandising (reach via LinkedIn or SpartanNash supplier portal)
- Start with 3–5 store pilot; propose Grand Rapids and West Michigan stores first
- SpartanNash's "Buy Michigan" program is active; align messaging

### 3. Meijer
**Type:** Regional hypermarket
**HQ:** Grand Rapids, Michigan (Walker, MI — 10 minutes from likely Hub location)
**Scale:** 250+ stores across MI, OH, IN, IL, KY, WI

**Food safety requirements (from Meijer Vendor Guide):** GFSI scheme preferred (SQF, PrimusGFS); minimum USDA GAP. Non-GMO seed/plant material required for produce. FSMA compliance mandatory. ([Meijer Vendor Requirements Guide](https://vendornet.meijer.com/DocumentView/Download?fileName=Guides%2FVendor+Requirements+and+Reference+Guide.pdf))

| Category | Spec | Volume | Approach |
|----------|------|--------|----------|
| Organic/local herbs | Clamshell 0.5–1 oz, PLU sticker, USDA Organic or non-GMO cert | 500–3,000 units/week | High-priority; Meijer has strong local produce commitment |
| Microgreens | 2–3 oz retail clamshell | 300–1,500 units/week | Specialty/natural section |
| Leafy greens (Phase 2) | 4–5 oz clamshell, SQF preferred | 5,000–20,000 units/week at full ramp | Long-term anchor buyer |

**Approach strategy:**
- Submit through Meijer Vendor Portal (vendornet.meijer.com); request pre-screening
- Meijer owned brand lead is based at 2350 3 Mile Rd N.W., Grand Rapids — this is a direct local relationship
- Meijer is likely to request a formal facility audit before first PO
- Timeline: Allow 4–6 months from first contact to first shipment

### 4. Corewell Health (formerly Spectrum Health)
**Type:** Healthcare system, hospital foodservice
**HQ:** Grand Rapids and Southfield, Michigan
**Largest Michigan-based health system**

**Active local purchasing:**
> "We work seasonally with local produce houses to bring us fresh Michigan produce." — Mick Rickerd, Executive Corporate Chef, Corewell Health ([Rapid Growth Media, 2024](https://rapidgrowthmedia.com/local-healthcare-systems-leverage-local-farms/))

Current local CEA suppliers include: Revolution Farms (Caledonia, MI) and **Square Roots (Wyoming, MI)**. The Hub is directly in their existing local CEA supply chain geography.

**Goal:** Purchase 25% of total food from local, sustainable, diverse suppliers by 2025 — on track per public statements.

| Category | Spec | Volume | Approach |
|----------|------|--------|----------|
| Herbs (fresh) | Bulk foodservice 5-lb or smaller; no clamshell needed for back-of-house | 20–100 lbs/week | Low barrier — direct farm sale possible without broker |
| Leafy greens | Bulk 5-lb case or 4-oz retail clamshell | 200–1,000 lbs/week | Preferred local over California |
| Microgreens | 1–5 lb bulk foodservice | 10–50 lbs/week | Chef-direct; culinary team innovation angle |
| Specialty items (edible flowers, saffron) | Small volume, per-order | As needed | R&D/farm tour tie-in |

**Approach strategy:**
1. Contact: Executive Corporate Chef Mick Rickerd directly (identified publicly); or through Corewell Food & Nutrition Services purchasing
2. No formal GFSI certification required for initial volumes; GAP preferred
3. Frame pitch around alignment with Corewell's sustainability + local food goals
4. Propose 3-month pilot with 1–2 SKUs; hospital kitchens need reliable weekly supply

### 5. Mercy Health (West Michigan)
**Type:** Healthcare system, hospital foodservice
**Coverage:** Multiple hospitals and clinics, West Michigan

**Context:** Michigan hospitals collectively signed the MHA Healthy Food Hospitals initiative pledging 20% local food sourcing by 2020 ([MSU CRFS Brief](https://www.canr.msu.edu/michiganfood/uploads/files/Agenda_Briefs_Priority8_InstPurchasing.pdf)). Mercy Health is a signatory.

| Category | Spec | Volume | Approach |
|----------|------|--------|----------|
| Fresh herbs | Bulk, foodservice | 10–50 lbs/week | Direct farm contact; reference Corewell precedent |
| Leafy greens | 4-oz clamshell or 5-lb bulk | 100–500 lbs/week | Phase 2 |

**Approach strategy:**
- Contact: Director of Food & Nutrition Services at each facility
- Reference Good Food for Michigan program ([Center for Good Food Purchasing](https://goodfoodpurchasing.org/good-food-for-michigan/)) as a shared framework
- Propose joint press release/media opportunity (local farm + local hospital = strong community story)

### 6. Whole Foods Market (Midwest Region)
**Type:** Specialty natural grocery
**Coverage:** Multiple Michigan locations; Midwest regional buyer based in Chicago area

**CEA precedent:** Square Roots supplied Whole Foods Michigan stores with indoor-grown herbs/microgreens ([Blue Book Services, 2021](https://www.bluebookservices.com/square-roots-to-sell-local-produce-in-spartannash-stores-in-michigan/))

| Category | Spec | Volume | Approach |
|----------|------|--------|----------|
| Organic/local herbs | 0.5–1 oz clamshell, Certified Organic preferred or non-GMO verified, sustainability claims | 300–1,000 units/week | Local Forager program — direct path for Michigan producers |
| Microgreens | 2–3 oz retail clamshell | 200–800 units/week | High-margin specialty section; health-conscious shopper |
| Specialty/premium items | Edible flowers, saffron-infused products | Small specialty SKUs | High-value, low-volume; supports brand positioning |

**Approach strategy:**
- Contact: Whole Foods Local Forager Program (regional buyer for Michigan)
- Requires: Third-party food safety audit (GAP minimum; SQF or PrimusGFS for volume)
- Whole Foods has strict non-GMO requirements; aligns with hub's positioning
- Allow 3–6 months from first contact to shelf

### 8. GVSU (Grand Valley State University) Dining
**Type:** University dining
**HQ:** Allendale, Michigan (20 miles from Grand Rapids)
**Status:** Active local food purchaser; 30%+ local spend; primary distributor is Gordon Food Service since 2014 ([GVSU Laker Food Co.](https://www.gvsu.edu/food/learn-more-156.htm))

| Category | Spec | Volume | Approach |
|----------|------|--------|----------|
| Herbs | Bulk foodservice | 10–50 lbs/week | Through GFS distribution (existing GVSU-GFS relationship) |
| Leafy greens | 5-lb bulk or 4-oz clamshell | 100–400 lbs/week | Phase 2; route through GFS |
| Microgreens | 1–5 lb bulk | 5–25 lbs/week | Chef innovation + sustainability messaging |

**Approach strategy:**
- Route through GFS (GVSU's preferred distributor) — **do not approach independently initially**
- GVSU also has Sustainable Agriculture Project (SAP) on campus; propose research/educational tie-in
- Frame around: student food experience, local supply chain education, campus sustainability goals

**HQ:** Kalamazoo, Michigan

**Status:** Goal to source 50% of food from local/regional producers; currently 30%+ local. Primary food hub: **ValleyHUB at KVCC** ([Rapid Growth Media, 2024](https://rapidgrowthmedia.com/local-healthcare-systems-leverage-local-farms/))

| Category | Spec | Volume | Approach |
|----------|------|--------|----------|
| Herbs | Bulk, 1–5 lb foodservice | 10–40 lbs/week | Route through ValleyHUB |
| Leafy greens | 5-lb foodservice or 4-oz retail | 100–400 lbs/week | Through ValleyHUB aggregation |

**Approach strategy:**
- Contact: ValleyHUB (Kalamazoo Valley Community College) — serves as primary local food aggregator for Bronson
