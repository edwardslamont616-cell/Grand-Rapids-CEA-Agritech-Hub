## Executive Summary

The Grand Rapids CEA Hub operates under a two-regulatory-track model:
1. **CEA produce track** (Zones A–D): FSMA Produce Safety Rule (21 CFR §112) + MDARD, with voluntary GFSI certification (SQF Level 2 or PrimusGFS) required for Meijer and Whole Foods access.
2. **Food processing track** (Zone E — soy processing): FDA 21 CFR Part 117 (Preventive Controls for Human Food), with mandatory allergen program, and SQF Food Manufacturing code.

Microgreens and sprouts (Zone A) carry heightened regulatory obligations under FSMA Subpart M (Sprouts).

## Track 1: CEA Produce (Zones A–D)

### Regulatory Framework

| Regulation | Authority | Scope | Compliance Status Target |
|-----------|-----------|-------|--------------------------|
| FSMA Produce Safety Rule (21 CFR §112) | FDA | Farms >\$25K/yr produce sales; covers growing, harvesting, packing, holding | **Year 1 — in place before first commercial sale** |
| MDARD Produce Safety Program | MDARD | Michigan implementation of federal PSR; state inspections | Register with MDARD; undergo On-Farm Readiness Review (OFRR) |
| USDA GAP/GHP Audit | USDA AMS | Voluntary but required by Meijer (minimum), GFS (minimum) | **Year 1 — before first retail sale to Meijer or GFS** |
| SQF Level 2 (Primary Plant Production) | SQF Institute (SQFI) | GFSI-recognized; required by Meijer preferred, Whole Foods | **Year 2 — after 1 year of documented production** |
| PrimusGFS | PrimusGFS (LGMA/SCS) | Alternate GFSI scheme; preferred by some foodservice chains | Optional — choose SQF OR PrimusGFS, not both |
| GlobalG.A.P. | GlobalG.A.P. | International; relevant if export or premium specialty retail targeted | Year 3+ if needed |

### FSMA Produce Safety Rule — Key Requirements

**Subpart E (Agricultural Water):**
- Municipal/public water supply: No pathogen testing required; obtain compliance certificates
- Record municipal water source; document any treatment
- Grand Rapids municipal water (GRWPC) = compliant source — minimal burden

**Subpart L (Biological Soil Amendments):**
- No raw manure in hydroponic CEA — N/A for this operation
- Document all fertilizer/nutrient sources

**Subpart O (Sprouts — CRITICAL for Zone A):**
> Microgreens are *not* legally classified as "sprouts" under FDA's Sprouts rule (sprouts = seeds germinated in water without medium; microgreens = seeded in substrate). However, operators should confirm their growing method clearly does not meet the sprouting definition.
- If any sprout products are grown (e.g., sunflower shoots in hydroponic water), Subpart M applies:
  - Spent irrigation water testing per production batch for *Salmonella* and *E. coli* O157:H7
  - Environmental monitoring for *Listeria* spp.
  - No product in commerce until negative results confirmed
- **Risk mitigation: Grow microgreens in substrate (coco coir, burlap, biostrate), not just water.** This maintains non-sprout classification and avoids Subpart M. ([FDA FSMA Produce Safety Rule](https://www.fda.gov/food/food-safety-modernization-act-fsma/fsma-final-rule-produce-safety))

**Worker Training:**
- All production workers must complete Produce Safety Alliance (PSA) Grower Training
- Cost: ~$25–$45/person online; annual refresher
- Training satisfies 21 CFR §112.22(c)

**Records:**
- Maintain for 2 years minimum
- Include: water testing, worker training, sanitation logs, harvest records

### MDARD Registration and Inspection

- Contact: MDARD Customer Service Center at 800-292-3939
- Request On-Farm Readiness Review (OFRR) — free, non-enforcement, helps identify gaps before official inspection
- Timeline: Request OFRR in Year 1 Q1; formal inspection expected in Q2–Q3

## Track 2: Soy Processing (Zone E) — FDA 21 CFR Part 117

### Regulatory Triggers

Zone E (tofu, soymilk, soy oil production) is **food manufacturing**, not farm production. This triggers:

1. **FDA facility registration** under the Bioterrorism Act (21 CFR §1.230+)
2. **21 CFR Part 117 — Current Good Manufacturing Practice, Hazard Analysis, and Risk-Based Preventive Controls for Human Food (PCHF rule)**
3. **Soy allergen program** — soy is one of the Big 9 allergens under FALCPA and FASTER Act 2021
4. **SQF Food Manufacturing code** (required by major retail buyers)

### FSMA PCHF (21 CFR Part 117) Key Requirements

| Requirement | Description | Timing |
|-------------|-------------|--------|
| Food Safety Plan (FSP) | Written hazard analysis + preventive controls | Before first production |
| HACCP program | Identify CCPs; establish critical limits, monitoring, corrective actions | Before first production |
| PCQI | Preventive Controls Qualified Individual must oversee FSP | Hire or train before production |
| cGMP (Subpart B) | Physical facility standards, personnel hygiene, pest control, equipment maintenance | Operational |
| Supply chain program | Document soybean sourcing (non-GMO certificate of analysis from each lot) | Ongoing |
| Allergen labeling program | Soy declaration on all finished product labels; dedicated equipment or validated cleaning if shared | Operational |

**PCQI Training:**
- Food Safety Preventive Controls Alliance (FSPCA) Preventive Controls for Human Food course: ~$700–$900/person; 2.5-day course
- PCQI certification required before Zone E opens

### Allergen Program (Soy)

Soy is a declared allergen under FALCPA (2004) and FASTER Act (2023, sesame added; soy remains in Big 9):
- All products must declare soy on label
- If any Zone B/C/D crops (herbs, greens, tomato) are packed in Zone E: **dedicated production runs or validated wet cleaning between allergen runs required**
- Strong recommendation: **physical separation between Zone E (soy processing) and Zones A–D (produce)** — separate facility space, separate HVAC, separate entry points

### SQF Food Manufacturing Code

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| SQF Code | Food Manufacturing ($650 for code; $800 if multiple codes) ([SQFI](https://www.sqfi.com/get-certified/register-for-sqf-certification)) |
| Audit cost | $8,000–$15,000 initial audit |
| Complexity vs. Produce | Higher — includes HACCP plan, allergen control, environmental monitoring program, supplier qualification |
| Timeline to certification | 12–18 months from first production |

## Risk Register

| Risk | Likelihood | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|-----------|--------|------------|
| Microgreens mis-classified as sprouts | Medium | High (requires Subpart M testing) | Use substrate; document growing method clearly |
| Water system contamination event | Low | High | Municipal source; maintain records; remediation plan |
| Allergen cross-contact (Zone E soy) | Medium | High | Physical separation; dedicated equipment; validated cleaning |
| SQF audit failure | Medium | Medium | Pre-assessment audit 6+ months prior |
| Staff PSA training lapse | Low | Medium | Annual refresher schedule; training records |
