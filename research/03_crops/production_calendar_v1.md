## Zone Architecture

| Zone | Name | Primary Crop(s) | System Type | Size (m²) Illustrative | Year Active |
|------|------|----------------|-------------|------------------------|-------------|
| A | Microgreens Production | Microgreens (broccoli, radish, sunflower, pea, basil) | Indoor vertical; 10×20 trays on wire racks | 200–400 m² | **Y1 Q1** |
| B | Leafy Greens & Herbs | Lettuce (butterhead, romaine), kale, spinach, bok choy, basil, cilantro, parsley, mint | Greenhouse NFT + DWC; some vertical | 1,000–2,000 m² | **Y1 Q2–Q3** |
| C | Greenhouse Tomato | Indeterminate tomato (cluster, cherry, heirloom) | Multi-span glasshouse, bumblebee pollination | 1,000–2,000 m² | **Y1 Q3–Q4** |
| D | Saffron Research & Tourism | Saffron (*Crocus sativus*) | Indoor CEA aeroponic/rack; visitor-accessible | 50–100 m² | **Y1 Q2** (establishment) |
| E | Processing / Value-Add | Soy processing (tofu, soymilk), herb drying/blending, product packaging | Food manufacturing space | 500–1,000 m² | **Y2** (separate regulatory track) |

> **Council note:** Zone E is classified as food processing (FDA 21 CFR Part 117), not CEA production. Its revenue is excluded from CEA production benchmarks.

## 52-Week Y1 Production Ramp (Quarters)

### Q1 (Weeks 1–13): Foundation

**Objective:** Zone A (microgreens) operational and selling; Zone B seeding; food safety program initiated.

| Week | Zone A (Microgreens) | Zone B (Greens/Herbs) | Zone C (Tomato) | Zone D (Saffron) | Zone E (Processing) |
|------|---------------------|----------------------|-----------------|-----------------|---------------------|
| 1–2 | Equipment install; seeding trials | Site prep; irrigation install | Structural build | — | Planning/permitting |
| 3–4 | First trays seeded (broccoli, radish, sunflower) | Transplant first lettuce | — | — | — |
| 5–6 | **First harvest** (radish ~8 days, sunflower ~9 days) | Lettuce continues | Greenhouse glazing | — | — |
| 7–8 | Regular production rhythm: 50–100 trays/week | First herb transplants (basil, cilantro) | Bumblebee colony sourced | — | — |
| 9–10 | Scale to 150–200 trays/week | First herb harvest (basil 28–35 days) | Tomato transplants | — | — |
| 11–12 | 200–250 trays/week | Herb sales to GFS, Corewell pilot | — | — | — |
| 13 | **Q1 revenue target: $15,000–$25,000** (microgreens + herbs) | Lettuce first harvest (Day 35–42) | — | Corm procurement begins | Regulatory pre-assessment |

**Y1 Q1 food safety milestones:**
- FSMA PSA Grower Training completed by all production staff
- GAP pre-assessment audit (internal)
- First water testing (municipal supply; establish records)

### Q2 (Weeks 14–26): Zone B Full Ramp + Zone D Establishment

**Objective:** Full leafy greens + herb production; first institutional sales; saffron corms planted; SQF pre-assessment.

| Week | Zone A | Zone B | Zone C | Zone D |
|------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| 14–16 | 300 trays/week | Lettuce at steady state; weekly harvest cycles | Foundation construction | Corms received; dark dormancy phase begins (90–100 days) |
| 17–19 | 350 trays/week; basil microgreens added | Herb varieties expanded (parsley, mint) | Greenhouse operational; tomato transplants begin | Corm dormancy continues |
| 20–22 | 400 trays/week (target capacity) | Lettuce sales to GFS, SpartanNash | Tomato plants vegetative growth | — |
| 23–26 | 400 trays/week maintained | 1,000–2,000 lbs/week herbs + leafy greens | First flowers appearing; bumblebees introduced | **Saffron flowering begins** (controlled dark-to-light transition) |

**Y1 Q2 targets:**
- Microgreens: 400 trays/week at ~$20/tray = $8,000/week gross
- Herbs + leafy greens: $5,000–$10,000/week
- **Q2 cumulative revenue: ~$130,000–$180,000**

### Q3 (Weeks 27–39): First Tomato Harvest; Zone E Planning

**Objective:** Greenhouse tomato first harvest; process and certify for Zone E; SQF Level 2 audit prep.

| Week | Zone A | Zone B | Zone C | Zone D |
|------|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| 27–30 | 400 trays/week steady | 2,000–3,000 lbs/week greens+herbs | First tomato clusters harvested (~90 days after transplant) | Saffron harvest window; hand-pick stigmas; dry for storage |
| 31–33 | Specialty varieties added (nasturtium, beet, chard microgreens) | Retailer onboarding: Meijer pilot | 500–1,000 lbs/week tomato | **Year 1 saffron yield: 50–100 g total (research/demonstration quantity)** |
| 34–36 | 450 trays/week | 3,000 lbs/week | 1,500–2,500 lbs/week tomato | Corm replanting for Y2 cycle |
| 37–39 | 450 trays/week | 3,500 lbs/week | 2,500–3,500 lbs/week tomato | Post-harvest corm storage |

**Q3 cumulative revenue: ~$200,000–$280,000**

### Q4 (Weeks 40–52): Stabilization + Y2 Planning

**Objective:** Full 4-zone CEA operation at steady state; Zone E permits in hand; SQF Level 2 certification.

| Week | Zone A | Zone B | Zone C | All Zones |
|------|--------|--------|--------|-----------|
| 40–44 | 450 trays/week | 4,000 lbs/week | 3,500–5,000 lbs/week | SQF Level 2 audit |
| 45–48 | 450 trays/week | 4,000 lbs/week | 4,000–5,500 lbs/week | Year 2 crop planning; Zone E final permitting |
| 49–52 | 450 trays/week | 4,500 lbs/week | 4,500–6,000 lbs/week | Y2 capital investment decisions |

**Q4 cumulative revenue: ~$300,000–$400,000**

**Y1 TOTAL CEA REVENUE ESTIMATE: $650,000–$900,000**

> **Honest framing (Council consensus):** A realistic Y1 CEA+processing revenue of $7–10M requires a significantly larger physical footprint than described above. The calendar above describes a single-campus, phased, conservative ramp appropriate for a first-year operation. The $14.6M original pro forma assumed full operations across all crop lines simultaneously — that is a Y3 scenario at best, and requires commensurate Phase 1 infrastructure investment.

## Weekly Production Rhythm (Steady State)

| Day | Activity |
|-----|---------|
| Monday | Seeding run (microgreens trays); transplant lettuce/herbs from propagation |
| Tuesday | Irrigation system check; nutrient solution testing (EC, pH all zones) |
| Wednesday | Microgreens harvest (trays seeded 8–10 days prior) |
| Thursday | Tomato harvest; herb cut-and-come-again harvest; packing and labeling |
| Friday | Delivery prep; quality inspection; reject count; IPM scouting all zones |
| Saturday | Minimal staffing; automated systems monitor; weekend market sales if applicable |
| Sunday | Deep-clean packing room; equipment maintenance; next-week planning |

## IPM Scouting Schedule (Integrated into Production Calendar)

| Frequency | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| Weekly | Full zone walk; yellow sticky trap count; Koppert swirskii sachet check |
| Bi-weekly | *Steinernema feltiae* drench to germination media (Zone A); Beauveria spray if thrips detected |
| Monthly | *Orius insidiosus* release (if whitefly pressure); zone environmental log review |
| Quarterly | External IPM consultant review; beneficial insect program efficacy assessment |
