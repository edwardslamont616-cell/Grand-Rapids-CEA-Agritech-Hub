# LED Fixture Shortlist — Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub
**Author:** Eng. Victor Ray, Controlled-Environment Lighting Engineer  
**Project:** Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub  
**Date:** 2025  
**Scope:** 8–12 commercial horticultural LED fixtures evaluated for Zone A (leafy greens/herbs/microgreens) and Zone B (greenhouse tomato supplemental)

---

## Selection Criteria

1. **Efficacy ≥ 2.7 µmol/J** (minimum for Tier 1; target ≥ 3.0 µmol/J for new procurement)
2. **DLC Horticultural listing** preferred; UL 8800 / ETL listed mandatory per NEC 2023 §410.172
3. **0–10V dimming** minimum; DALI or networked control preferred for Zone A load-flex
4. **IP66** or better for wet horticultural environment
5. Form factor appropriate to mounting height: bar/strip for multi-tier (Zone A); high-output fixture for greenhouse (Zone B)
6. **≥ 5-year warranty** (50,000 hr L90 lifetime target)

## Zone Mapping and Recommendation Summary

### Zone A — Leafy Greens / Herbs / Microgreens (Multi-tier vertical)

**Recommended primary:** Signify GreenPower LED Toplighting Linear (Compact) or Valoya LL-Series  
**Rationale:** Efficacy 3.2–3.9 µmol/J is the highest available tier. For 6-layer racks at close mounting heights (<30 cm canopy to fixture), the linear bar form factor minimizes hot-spots and maximizes uniformity. Valoya LL-Series IP65, IP66 not confirmed — verify with vendor. Signify has CSA/UL listing, which simplifies AHJ approval in Michigan.

**Recommended control protocol:** DALI-2 (Signify TLF) or 0–10V (all fixtures) networked to zone BMS. For load-flex operation (24 L:0 D), 0–10V is adequate. DALI enables per-luminaire addressability and logging.

**Alternative:** Fluence SPYDR 2i at 2.7 µmol/J is a well-proven multi-tier fixture with IP66, UL 8800; suitable as primary or backup.

### Zone B — Greenhouse Tomato (Supplemental toplighting)

**Recommended primary:** Signify GreenPower LED Toplighting Force (TLF) 4-channel  
**Rationale:** Efficacy 3.9–4.3 µmol/J is best-in-class for greenhouse toplighting. 4-channel spectral control (R:G:B:FR) allows dynamic spectrum recipe for tomato stages. High-output (2,650–5,150 µmol/s per fixture family) reduces fixture count. DALI-2 enables per-row dimming.

**Alternative:** Fluence VYPR 2p at 2.7 µmol/J, UL 8800; established track record in greenhouse tomato.

## Notes on DLC Listing

The DesignLights Consortium (DLC) maintains a [Horticultural Lighting Qualified Products List (QPL)](https://www.designlights.org/horticultural-lighting/). As of 2025, DLC listing requires minimum PPE of 1.7 µmol/J (Tier 1) or 2.1 µmol/J (Tier 2). All fixtures with ≥ 2.7 µmol/J exceed DLC Tier 2 requirements. Confirm DLC listing status at time of procurement — this affects eligibility for Michigan utility rebates and ASHRAE 90.1 compliance pathway.

**Fixtures confirmed or likely DLC-listed:** Signify GreenPower (confirmed UL/CSA), Fluence SPYDR 2i (UL 8800 confirmed), Gavita Pro 1700e (ETL).  
**Fixtures requiring DLC verification:** Heliospectra MITRA/ELIXIA, Valoya LL-Series, Current Arize Lynk Gen 2 (older gen confirmed DLC; Gen 3 status TBC), California LightWorks SolarSystem 1100.
