# Draft Outreach: Downtown Grand Rapids Inc. (DGRI)
**From:** Jordan Rivers, Hospitality & Agri-Tourism Specialist, Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub
**To:** Executive Leadership, Downtown Grand Rapids Inc.
**Subject:** Introducing the CEA Agritech Hub — A GR Forward Project Arrives on the Grand River
**Tone:** Civic-partner tone; respectful of DGRI's role; collaborative, not supplicant

---

Dear Downtown Grand Rapids Inc. Leadership Team,

We are building something that I believe belongs in the same sentence as GR Forward.

The **Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub** is a hydro-powered, year-round controlled environment agriculture campus sited on the Grand River corridor in downtown Grand Rapids. We grow food with renewable energy from the river. We host tens of thousands of guests annually. We serve school groups, corporate teams, convention delegates, and curious residents. And we are preparing to open our doors at a moment when the Grand River itself is being restored to what it once was.

I'm writing to introduce ourselves, share what we're building, and ask for the opportunity to coordinate with DGRI as we approach our public opening.

**Alignment with GR Forward**

The [GR Forward initiative](https://downtowngr.org/our-work/major-collaborations) — led by DGRI in partnership with the City, Grand Rapids Public Schools, and Grand Rapids Whitewater — established a community vision for transforming the Grand River corridor into a distinct economic, ecological, and social asset. The Hub is a physical expression of that vision:

- **Economic:** 40,000–60,000 annual guest experiences; direct hospitality revenue; support for local food vendors and hospitality workforce
- **Ecological:** Hydro-electric power from the Grand River; closed-loop water systems; apiary supporting local pollination ecology
- **Social:** K–12 field trips (aligned to NGSS science standards); adult workforce education; free/subsidized community nights; ADA-accessible programming

We are also directly positioned to benefit from — and contribute to — the Lower Grand River restoration. [Grand Rapids Whitewater's dam removal and channel reconstruction](https://grandrapidswhitewater.org/the-project/), with in-river construction beginning in 2025, will activate a new cohort of active-leisure visitors to the corridor. The Hub is a year-round indoor complement to the outdoor river experience: when visitors finish a paddle or a riverfront walk, we want them to step inside and see where their food comes from.

**What We're Requesting**

We would welcome the opportunity to:

1. **Coordinate on corridor placemaking:** Discuss wayfinding, signage, or pedestrian connections between the Hub site, the riverfront, and downtown's core (Calder Plaza, Monroe Center, the Market District corridor). The Downtown Ambassadors program — which made [150,000+ guest assists in 2022](https://www.experiencegr.com/meetings-blog/post/downtown-ambassadors/) — is a natural channel for directing curious visitors our way.

2. **Explore World of Winter integration:** The Hub's glowing grow rooms and illuminated apiary observation area present a compelling World of Winter pop-up opportunity. We'd love to discuss what a collaborative WoW activation might look like.

3. **Connect with GRPS and education partners:** DGRI's relationships with Grand Rapids Public Schools are something we deeply respect. We are building a K–12 school field trip program aligned to Michigan's science standards, and would welcome a warm introduction to the district's curriculum coordinators.

4. **Brief DGRI leadership:** We'd welcome 30 minutes with your team for a formal introduction and site preview when our facility is ready for visitors.

5. **Access to downtown vitals / foot-traffic data:** If DGRI's pedestrian counter data (from your ArtPrize sidewalk traffic studies) covers the Grand River corridor near our planned site, we would be grateful for any guidance on how to access or interpret that data as we model our visitation projections.

The downtown Grand Rapids story is one of the best in American city-building. We want to add a chapter.

Thank you for the work you do and for the city you've helped shape. We'd be honored to be part of what comes next.

With respect and excitement,
