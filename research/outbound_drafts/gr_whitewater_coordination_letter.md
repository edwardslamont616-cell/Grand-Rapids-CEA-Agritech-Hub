Dear Executive Director,

I am writing on behalf of the **CEA Campus Design Council**, the development and planning team for the proposed Grand Rapids CEA (Controlled-Environment Agriculture) Agritech Hub at **555 Monroe Ave NE, Grand Rapids, Michigan** — a 100,000 square-foot vertical agriculture facility sited adjacent to the Grand River, immediately within or near the Lower Reach project corridor.

First, I want to extend our sincere congratulations on reaching the fully-funded, fully-permitted milestone for the Grand River Revitalization. The NRCS authorization of $11,026,695 in March 2026, combined with the City's $14.56 million construction contract with Taplin Group, represents the culmination of nearly two decades of extraordinary community leadership by your organization and your partners. The restoration of the Grand River is a transformational project for our city.

We are writing now because our project and your project will be neighbors — both physically along the riverbank and in terms of the shared goal of making the Grand River an asset for economic vitality, ecological health, and community resilience in Grand Rapids.

### Why We Are Reaching Out

The CEA Campus Design Council is evaluating long-term energy options for the Agritech Hub, including the potential for a **small run-of-river hydroelectric installation** using the Grand River. We want to be transparent with Grand Rapids Whitewater about this planning activity, and we want to ensure that anything we explore in future years is designed **in coordination with — not in opposition to — the vision your organization has championed**.

We have reviewed your project documentation carefully, and we understand the following:

- The Lower Reach project removes four dangerous low-head dams and replaces them with natural grade control structures designed by EGLE and MDNR to restore rapids and aquatic habitat. **Construction begins summer 2026.**
- The grade control structures are engineered to provide natural riffle character, not hydraulic impoundment. We do not assume any head availability for a turbine from these structures without your team's and EGLE's explicit input.
- The federally endangered snuffbox mussel (*Epioblasma triquetra*) — nearly 10,000 specimens, including 38 federally endangered individuals — was relocated from this reach in 2024. Any future activity that could affect this population carries the highest level of ESA scrutiny.
- The Upper Reach EIS (6th Street Dam / sea lamprey barrier) is now in active NEPA scoping. We understand the complexity of this regulatory environment.

**We are not proposing any in-channel installation.** We will not proceed with any structure that conflicts with the restoration project, disrupts the grade control structures, or imposes any impact on the restored aquatic habitat. Our interest is in a prospective long-term (post-2028) side-channel bypass concept — and only if such a concept can be designed in a manner that is fully compatible with your restoration goals and the conditions established by EGLE, USACE, USFWS, and MDNR.

### What We Are Requesting

We respectfully request a coordination meeting of approximately one hour — at your offices, at the CEA Campus site, or via video conference — to accomplish the following:

1. **Introduce our project team and site context.** We want Grand Rapids Whitewater to know who we are, what we are building, and why we see the river as central to our mission.

2. **Understand your design constraints.** We would appreciate an opportunity to learn about the grade control structure designs, the hydraulic modeling that informed them, and any conditions — structural, ecological, or operational — that a future side-channel bypass concept would need to respect.

3. **Identify potential alignment.** Our facility includes educational programming for K-12 and higher education students on food systems, water science, and engineering. We see significant potential for partnership with your outdoor education programs, your Lower Grand River Organization of Watersheds partnership, and your river science initiatives.

4. **Establish an ongoing communication channel.** We want to be a good neighbor throughout your construction period (2026–2028 and beyond) and to position ourselves as a supportive community stakeholder, not a complicating factor.

### About the CEA Campus

The Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub is a proposed 100,000 square-foot, six-layer controlled-environment agriculture facility that will produce leafy greens, herbs, and specialty crops using precision LED lighting, hydroponic growing systems, and integrated pest management. It is being developed as an Opportunity Zone investment with the goal of creating local food system resilience, STEM-focused education programming, and agri-tourism opportunities in Grand Rapids.

We share Grand Rapids Whitewater's conviction that the Grand River is the defining natural asset of our city, and we want our facility to honor that.
