Dear [Director Last Name] and Team,

I am writing on behalf of the Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub to introduce our organization and to propose a formal workforce development partnership with West Michigan Works!. We believe this relationship can serve mutual goals: the Hub needs trained workers in an emerging occupation not yet well-served by existing pipelines; and West Michigan Works! serves job seekers, returning citizens, and adult learners who need exactly the kind of meaningful, well-paying career pathway that Hub employment can offer.

**About the Hub**

The Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub is a hydro-powered, controlled-environment agriculture (CEA) campus in development in Grand Rapids. CEA — growing food in highly controlled indoor environments using hydroponics and precision lighting — is one of the fastest-growing food production sectors in the country, driven by food security concerns, water conservation imperatives, and consumer demand for locally grown produce year-round.

When fully operational, the Hub will employ 15–25 full-time and part-time workers in roles including CEA Operators, Lead Growers, Apiary Technicians, Facilities/Energy Technicians, Education Coordinators, and Hospitality/Retail staff. Hub wages start at $17–$20/hour for entry-level operators, with a structured 18-month advancement pathway.

Critically, Hub leadership — including founder Lamont Edwards — has made a public commitment to hiring from communities that face structural employment barriers, including returning citizens, adults seeking career transitions, and individuals from West Michigan's underserved neighborhoods.

**What We Are Proposing**

*1. Going PRO Talent Fund Application (Immediate Priority)*
The Hub would like to apply for the Michigan Going PRO Talent Fund for the upcoming cycle (Cycle 2, April 24, 2026 deadline ([michigan.gov/leo](https://www.michigan.gov/leo/bureaus-agencies/wd/programs-services/going-pro-talent-fund))). We are seeking West Michigan Works!' guidance and co-signature in developing an application to fund training for 10–15 new Hub hires through the GRCC CEA Operations Certificate program. We believe this is a strong application: new occupational category (no existing incumbent worker training program), high-demand sector, direct connection to employer hiring commitment, and GRCC as an approved training provider.

*2. On-the-Job Training (OJT) Employer Registration*
We request assistance registering the Hub as an approved OJT employer in the WMW! system. With OJT agreements in place, new Hub hires can receive up to 50% wage reimbursement during the initial training period — significantly reducing the Hub's labor cost burden in the critical startup phase while ensuring workers receive paid, structured on-site training alongside the GRCC certificate coursework.

*3. Individual Training Account (ITA) — Approved Training Provider*
We request that the GRCC CEA Operations Certificate be added to WMW!'s approved training program list so that ITA vouchers ($3,000–$8,000 per eligible adult) can be applied toward tuition. This will open the certificate to WMW!'s roster of dislocated workers, adult learners, and career-changers who currently lack funded pathways into the food systems sector.

*4. Second-Chance Hiring Pipeline*
Hub leadership has expressed specific commitment to hiring returning citizens. We are seeking WMW!'s guidance on:
- Connecting with MDOC reentry coordinators and local reentry organizations (Degage Ministries, Access of West Michigan) whose clients may be appropriate candidates for Hub enrollment and employment
- Using the Federal Bonding Program (USDOL) to bond Hub employees who are returning citizens, reducing employer risk
- Structuring the Hub's hiring policy in alignment with Michigan's Clean Slate Act (PA 123 of 2020) and EEOC individual-assessment guidance

We envision a dedicated cohort of 4–6 second-chance participants in each Hub certificate cohort, with a clear pathway from certificate completion to paid Hub employment.

*5. Sector Partnership Table*
We propose co-convening a West Michigan CEA / Food Systems Sector Partnership Table — bringing together the Hub, GRCC, other regional greenhouse and indoor farming employers, and food processing companies — to develop a shared competency framework and advocate jointly for workforce training funding. This table would strengthen future Going PRO applications and ensure the Hub's certificate curriculum remains aligned with actual regional employer needs.

*6. Michigan Reconnect Coordination*
We are working with GRCC to ensure the CEA Operations Certificate meets the 16-credit threshold for Michigan Reconnect tuition-free eligibility ([michigan.gov/reconnect](https://www.michigan.gov/reconnect)). WMW! is a key touchpoint for Reconnect-eligible adults who may not know the certificate exists. We would welcome inclusion of the Hub program in WMW! career advising materials and orientation sessions.
