Dear Dean [Last Name],

I am writing on behalf of the Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub — a hydro-powered, controlled-environment agriculture campus currently in development in Grand Rapids — to explore a formal educational and research partnership with Grand Valley State University's College of Liberal Arts and Sciences and the Brooks College of Interdisciplinary Studies.

**About the Hub**

The Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub integrates micro-hydro electric power generation, commercial hydroponic food production, an educational apiary, and a three-tier education platform spanning K-12 field programming, community college workforce credentials, and higher education research. Our development team includes engineers, horticulturists, educators, and community development professionals committed to making West Michigan a national model for community-accessible, sustainable food production.

We are adjacent to GVSU's Grand Rapids campus, making practical and logistical collaboration straightforward.

**Why GVSU**

Grand Valley's interdisciplinary mission — and specifically Brooks College's emphasis on integrative, community-engaged scholarship — aligns precisely with the kind of work the Hub is designed to support. We are not seeking a traditional contract research arrangement; we are proposing a long-term partnership in which GVSU students and faculty use the Hub as a living laboratory for research, capstone projects, and community engagement, and the Hub's operations benefit from GVSU's intellectual capacity.

**Specific Opportunities We Are Proposing**

*1. Capstone Project Hosting (immediate, AY 2026–27)*
The Hub can host 4–8 interdisciplinary capstone projects annually, providing on-site access, data, and staff mentorship. We have developed five project prompts ready for review (full descriptions available upon request), spanning:
- IoT environmental sensor network design (Padnos College Engineering)
- Food security equity analysis and CSA model development (Brooks/CLAS Social Sciences)
- Pollinator health monitoring dashboard (Biology/Environmental Studies)
- Workforce pipeline impact study — CEA certificate completers (Social Sciences/Education)
- Lifecycle assessment of local vs. long-supply-chain produce (Environmental Studies/Engineering)

Each project produces a deliverable of practical value to Hub operations, and students gain authentic, professional-level field research experience.

*2. Sponsored Research Partnership (AY 2027–28 and beyond)*
We are identifying research topics where GVSU faculty could serve as principal investigators on externally funded grants, with the Hub as the primary research site. Of immediate interest: a low-cost IoT food safety sensor network for FSMA Produce Safety Rule compliance in small CEA operations — a project we believe is competitive for an NSF SBIR Phase I award or USDA NIFA AFRI grant. We are seeking a GVSU Padnos College engineering faculty member as PI; the Hub provides the physical infrastructure, operational context, and extension outreach component.

*3. Student Internship and Practicum Pathway*
We are prepared to offer paid internships to GVSU students in biology, environmental science, public health, and engineering beginning in Y1 of Hub operation. These can be structured as academic-year or summer experiences, with clear learning objectives and faculty supervisor coordination.

*4. Co-Hosted Community Lecture Series*
Given GVSU's presence in Grand Rapids and the Hub's educational mission, we propose a joint "Food, Energy, and Water" public lecture series — one event per semester — held at the Hub and open to the Grand Rapids community. GVSU faculty present research; Hub provides the experiential context.

**What We Are Asking**

We request a 30-minute introductory meeting at your earliest convenience to explore mutual interest and identify which of these opportunities are the best fit for your current strategic priorities. Following that conversation, we would be prepared to draft a formal Memorandum of Understanding outlining roles, expectations, and IP provisions.

The Hub is scheduled to begin operations in [anticipated opening year], and we are building our academic partnership network now, so that curriculum integration and research protocols are ready when our first cohort of students and researchers arrives.
