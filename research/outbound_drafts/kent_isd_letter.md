**About the Hub**

The Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub is a hydro-powered, controlled-environment agriculture campus located in Grand Rapids. When fully operational, the Hub will produce leafy greens, herbs, and specialty crops using hydroponic systems powered in part by on-site micro-hydro generation. The campus also includes a functioning apiary with education programming, a post-harvest kitchen, and dedicated educational spaces designed to serve school groups, community college students, and university researchers.

The Hub's education team has designed a complete K-12 program aligned to Michigan's K-12 Science Standards (aligned with NGSS, for which Michigan was a Lead State — [nextgenscience.org/lead-state-michigan](https://www.nextgenscience.org/lead-state-michigan)) and the MDE CTE Agriculture, Food & Natural Resources cluster.

**What We Are Proposing**

*1. "River → Plate" — 8th Grade Interdisciplinary Unit (Ready for Pilot AY 2026-27)*
We have developed a full 6-week, Understanding by Design unit for 8th-grade science, anchored to four NGSS Performance Expectations:
- **MS-LS2-1** (ecosystem resource availability → organism populations)
- **MS-LS2-3** (matter cycling and energy flow)
- **MS-ESS3-3** (scientific principles for monitoring and minimizing human environmental impact)
- **MS-ETS1-1** (engineering design criteria and constraints)

The unit — "River → Plate" — traces water from the Grand River through a CEA facility to a student's lunch tray. It integrates Common Core Math (proportional reasoning, statistical analysis, linear functions) and ELA (CER writing, informational text). All materials are teacher-ready, including daily lesson plans, data tables, materials lists, a summative assessment rubric, and weekly family communications.

The unit includes two site visits to the Hub, which we offer at a subsidized rate for Kent ISD Title I schools.

We are seeking 2–3 pilot classrooms in Kent County for AY 2026–27, with full teacher support provided by Hub education staff.

*2. Elementary and High School Programming*
- **Discovery Day Field Trips (Grades 3–5):** 3-hour guided experience covering plant life cycles, pollinator biology, and basic hydroponic observation. Aligned to NGSS 3-LS1-1, 3-LS2-1.
- **High School CTE Integration:** For schools with an Agriculture, Food & Natural Resources CTE pathway, we offer a semester-long structured CTE experience for 10–20 students per year, aligned to Michigan's Plant Systems and Environmental Service Systems pathway standards. Students earn CTE experience hours and Hub will explore articulation agreements with GRCC.

*3. Teacher Professional Development (Summer 2026)*
We are offering a 2-day CEA Educator Workshop for Kent ISD science and CTE teachers, covering:
- Hands-on hydroponic system setup and operation
- NGSS-aligned inquiry design for CEA contexts
- How to use Hub site visits as anchor phenomena for classroom units
- Access to Hub's curriculum materials repository

This workshop counts toward MDE Continuing Education hours. Enrollment limited to 20 teachers per cohort.
