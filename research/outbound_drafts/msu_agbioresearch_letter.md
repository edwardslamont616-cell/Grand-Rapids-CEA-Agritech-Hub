**About the Hub**

The Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub is a 20,000–40,000 sq ft controlled-environment agriculture facility integrated with micro-hydro electric generation, an educational apiary, and a three-tier education platform (K-12, community college certificates, and higher education). When operational, the Hub will maintain commercial-scale hydroponic lettuce, herb, and microgreen production alongside dedicated research bays available to university partners. We expect to host 2–4 active research pilots annually.

**The Opportunity: A Sponsored Research Partnership**

We are proposing to establish the Hub as a formal MSU AgBioResearch satellite research site — not a passive host, but an active partner that contributes infrastructure, staff support, community extension reach, and real-world commercial context that laboratory environments cannot replicate.

We have identified two pilot research projects where your expertise and MSU's research infrastructure would be the ideal fit:

**Pilot 1: Dynamic LED Spectrum × Lettuce Yield and Nutritional Quality**
Building directly on OptimIA findings regarding far-red and blue light effects, this pilot would test a dynamic spectrum protocol — transitioning from blue-dominant in early growth stages to balanced red:far-red in the final two weeks — in a commercial-scale DWC system at the Hub. We would measure fresh weight yield, canopy expansion, ORAC antioxidant value, and nitrate content at harvest, and calculate energy cost per kilogram under each protocol. This is a natural extension of your existing LED spectrum research and could generate the data package needed for a competitive USDA SCRI SREP application ("Optimizing Dynamic LED Protocols for Nutritionally Enhanced Indoor Lettuce in Northern U.S. Markets"). Estimated budget: $55,000 over 12 months.

**Pilot 3: Hydroponic Nutrient Regime × Microgreen Nutrient Density**
This pilot would examine how varying nitrogen source (ammonium vs. nitrate) and EC levels affect Vitamin C and Vitamin E content in sunflower and pea shoot microgreens. Given Dr. Valle de Souza's work on indoor crop economics and your team's consumer attribute research, we believe this has direct application to positioning high-value microgreens for regional food service and institutional buyers. Estimated budget: $53,000 over 12 months.

**What the Hub Provides**
- Dedicated research bays (4 × 100 sq ft) with full environmental control and data logging
- Hub staff support for daily data collection and plant care
- Commercial crop production context for extension outreach deliverables
- Community education extension to Hub's K-12 and certificate program audiences
- F&A recovery to both Hub and MSU at negotiated rates

**What We Request from MSU**
- PI leadership and experimental design oversight
- Graduate Research Associate (0.5 FTE) on funded pilot
- Co-authorship and peer-reviewed publication commitment
- Co-submission of follow-on USDA SCRI or AFRI proposal naming Hub as project site

**The Precedent**
Your own research trajectory — from Project GREEEN seed funding to a $2.7M USDA SCRI award for OptimIA, and then a $3.4M SCRI HERB grant — demonstrates exactly the pathway we are seeking to replicate. The Hub is designed to be the kind of site-based partner that makes a compelling SCRI application possible: real production scale, community extension reach, industry relevance, and data continuity across seasons.

**Proposed Next Steps**

I would welcome a 30-minute video call at your convenience to discuss whether this partnership aligns with your current research priorities and any active or planned grant applications. If there is interest, we can follow up with a draft Research Use Agreement for your review.

The Hub begins operations in [year], and we are establishing research partnerships now so that the first pilot can begin within 60 days of our opening.
