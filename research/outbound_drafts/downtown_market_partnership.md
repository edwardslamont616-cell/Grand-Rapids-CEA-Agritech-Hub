# Draft Outreach: Downtown Market Grand Rapids Partnership
**From:** Jordan Rivers, Hospitality & Agri-Tourism Specialist, Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub
**To:** Leadership Team, Downtown Market Grand Rapids
**Subject:** Partnership Opportunity — Growing Something Together | Introducing the CEA Agritech Hub
**Tone:** Collegial, food-community-first, collaborative

---

Dear Downtown Market Grand Rapids Team,

I have long admired what you've built at 435 Ionia Avenue — a living, breathing hub of food culture that opened with [8,000–10,000 visitors on a single Saturday](https://rapidgrowthmedia.com/dwntnmkt0509/) and hasn't stopped drawing people in since. The Downtown Market has defined what "local food destination" means in West Michigan, and that's exactly the community we want to join.

I'm reaching out on behalf of the **Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub**, a new downtown facility that will grow food year-round inside a hydro-powered controlled environment agriculture campus on the Grand River corridor. We're preparing for our public opening and believe the Downtown Market and the Hub are natural partners — two food-forward institutions that, together, could offer Grand Rapids the beginning of a genuine urban food trail.

**Where We Complement Each Other**

| Downtown Market | CEA Agritech Hub |
|----------------|-----------------|
| 21 food merchants; prepared, retail, specialty food | Living crops growing in real time; farm-to-table direct |
| Food destination with retail/dining focus | Immersive experience (tours, workshops, dinners) |
| Outdoor and indoor market events | Year-round indoor programming (weather-independent) |
| Hub of West Michigan's local food movement | Source of locally grown produce and honey |

We are not in competition. We are a farm and you are the marketplace.

**Partnership Ideas We'd Love to Explore**

1. **Ingredient partnership:** Hub-grown herbs, microgreens, and honey supplied to select Downtown Market vendors — with co-branded "Grown at the Hub" signage at point of sale. This gives vendors a local provenance story and gives the Hub visibility with your hundreds of weekly shoppers.

2. **Cross-promotional ticketing:** Hub tour brochures and QR codes available at the Market's information desk; Downtown Market Merchant Guide available in our welcome lounge. A "Saturday Food Loop" itinerary — morning at the Market, afternoon at the Hub — creates a natural day-experience pairing for visitors.

3. **Joint "Urban Food Trail" map:** A self-guided printed map and digital itinerary connecting the Market, the Hub, and key corridor partners (Grand River Whitewater, Experience Grand Rapids). West Michigan food tourism infrastructure that didn't exist before.

4. **Collaborative events:** A once-per-season dinner or tasting event co-produced by a Downtown Market chef and the Hub team. "Grown 2 miles from where it's cooked" is a compelling story.

5. **Pop-up presence:** We'd love to bring Hub-grown samples and membership enrollment to an outdoor market date (Saturday) in the weeks before our opening — pure community-building, no hard sell.

**What We're Asking**

A 30-minute coffee or call to share what we're building and see where the conversation goes. We believe the relationship between the Market and the Hub can become one of Grand Rapids' best food-system partnerships, and we'd rather start that conversation early than arrive after we're already open.

Thank you for the community you've created. We're excited to grow beside you.

Warmly,
