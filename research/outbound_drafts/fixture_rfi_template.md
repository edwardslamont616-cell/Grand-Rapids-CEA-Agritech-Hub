## 1. Project Background

The Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub is a 100,000 SF mixed-use controlled-environment agriculture (CEA) campus incorporating:
- **Zone A:** Six-layer indoor vertical farm for leafy greens, herbs, and microgreens (~22,300 m² total canopy)
- **Zone B:** ~2,000 m² glass greenhouse for fruiting tomato (supplemental toplighting)
- **Zone C:** Microgreens sub-zone within Zone A
- Visitor center, soy processing, and apiary support zones

The facility is powered by a run-of-river hydroelectric system with grid interconnection. Lighting represents the dominant energy load (~61% of total facility consumption), and load-flex capability is a primary design objective.

This RFI is issued to three (3) LED fixture suppliers for technical, commercial, and compliance information to inform fixture selection for the Base Bid. Responses are non-binding but will directly inform fixture specifications in the 60% Design Documents.

## 2. Requested Fixture Categories

**Category 1 — Zone A Multi-tier VF Bar/Strip Fixture**  
Form factor: Linear bar, 4 ft or 8 ft, for mounting on growing rack structure at 15–30 cm above canopy  
Target efficacy: ≥ 3.0 µmol/J (preferred); minimum 2.7 µmol/J  
Target PPFD at canopy: 170 µmol/m²/s (for 24 L:0 D operation) or 250 µmol/m²/s (for 16 L:8 D operation)  
Quantity estimate: **~5,000–7,000 fixtures** (subject to final photometric layout)

**Category 2 — Zone B Greenhouse Toplighting Fixture**  
Form factor: High-output greenhouse toplighting fixture; linear or high-bay  
Target efficacy: ≥ 3.5 µmol/J  
Target installed intensity: 300 W/m² (250–300 µmol/m²/s supplemental PPFD at 1.5 m mounting height above crop)  
Quantity estimate: **~200–350 fixtures**
