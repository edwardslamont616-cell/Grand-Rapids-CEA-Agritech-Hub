### Proposed Generation Systems

We are evaluating two potential DER installations, either independently or in combination. Both are anticipated to fall within the **Level 3 category (150–550 kW)** under Consumers Energy's current Generator Interconnection Procedures (Rev. October 2, 2025, MPSC Case No. U-20890):

**Option A — Rooftop/Ground-Mount Solar PV + Battery Energy Storage System (BESS)**  
- Proposed PV system: 400–750 kW AC nameplate capacity  
- Proposed BESS: 1,000–2,000 kWh usable capacity, 500–1,000 kW power rating  
- Operating mode: Export (net billing) or Non-Export (subject to technical review)  
- Technology: Inverter-based; IEEE 1547-2018 compliant equipment (Category II or III)  
- Interconnection voltage: Subject to Consumers Energy determination; likely 480V or 4.8 kV depending on feeder  

**Option B — Run-of-River Hydroelectric Turbine**  
- Proposed capacity: ~400 kW  
- Technology: Synchronous or induction generator (turbine type TBD)  
- Location: Grand River frontage adjacent to or at 555 Monroe Ave NE  
- Operating mode: Export (continuous baseload, subject to river flow)  
- Note: This option is subject to federal (FERC) and state (EGLE) permitting and may not be pursued in Phase 1.

We anticipate initially proceeding with **Option A** (solar PV + BESS) and request guidance on the Level 3 interconnection process for this configuration.

### Specific Questions — Pre-Application Inquiry

Pursuant to your Level 3 Generator Interconnection Procedures, we respectfully request responses to the following questions to inform our application and system design:

1. **Feeder identification and capacity:** What distribution feeder(s) serve 555 Monroe Ave NE, and what is the current available capacity for additional DER interconnection? Is a pre-application report required at this stage?

2. **Track assignment:** Given a proposed PV+BESS system of 400–750 kW AC with export capability, which interconnection track would apply — Non-Export, Fast Track, or Study Track? Would a certified inverter-based system qualify for the Fast Track initial review screens?

3. **IEEE 1547-2018 compliance requirements:** Consumers Energy's October 2025 procedures reference the 2023 Michigan Interconnection and Distributed Generation Standards (MPSC Case No. U-20890). What performance category (Category I, II, or III) is required for an exporting DER at this location? Are there specific inverter certification or anti-islanding requirements for this feeder?

4. **Metering and telemetry requirements:** What metering, protection relay, and communications/telemetry equipment is required for a Level 3 exporting system? Is smart metering (AMI) already installed at this service address?

5. **Net billing tariff:** Is the facility eligible for the Consumers Energy net billing program? What is the current net billing compensation rate, and are there capacity or queue limitations?

6. **Hydro interconnection (Option B — future inquiry):** If a run-of-river hydroelectric turbine (synchronous generator, ~400 kW) were interconnected at this site in a future phase, what additional requirements — protective relay settings, power factor control, harmonic analysis — would apply compared to the inverter-based PV system? What certification documentation would be required for a non-certified synchronous generator?

7. **Combined DER aggregate capacity:** If both PV and hydro systems were ultimately installed at this site, what aggregate DER capacity rating would apply, and how would level determination work for a combined interconnection application?
