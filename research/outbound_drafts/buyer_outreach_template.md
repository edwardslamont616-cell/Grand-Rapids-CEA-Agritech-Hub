## Usage Instructions

These templates are designed for first outreach to Michigan/Midwest category buyers. Select the appropriate version by buyer type. Personalize the bracketed fields. All templates are intentionally brief — category buyers receive many vendor pitches and respond to specificity and brevity.

---

## Template 1: Retail Category Buyer (Meijer, SpartanNash, Whole Foods)

**Subject:** Local CEA Herbs + Microgreens — Year-Round Supply, [City], MI

---

Hi [First Name],

I'm [Name], [Title] at the Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub — a new controlled-environment agriculture campus located [X miles] from [Buyer HQ/Distribution Center].

We grow fresh herbs (basil, cilantro, parsley, mint) and specialty microgreens in a hydroponic, climate-controlled facility with zero pesticides, non-GMO inputs, and year-round consistent supply.

**Why this might matter to your produce set:**
- **15-day+ shelf life advantage** over field-grown product shipped from California or Mexico
- **USDA GAP certified** (SQF Level 2 in Year 2); non-GMO; [organic or conventional]
- Product available for **52-week supply** — no seasonal gaps
- Local sourcing story: grown [X miles] from your nearest distribution center

**What I'm proposing:**
A 6-week pilot with 1–2 SKUs at [2–5 stores / your discretion] — no long-term commitment, just enough to validate sell-through.

I've attached a one-page product spec sheet. Would a 20-minute call work this week or next to discuss?

[Name]
[Title], Grand Rapids CEA Agritech Hub
[Email] | [Phone]
[Address, Grand Rapids area, MI]

---

*Personalization notes for specific buyers:*

**Meijer:** Mention that Meijer's vendor requirements specify non-GMO seed/plant material — confirm alignment. Reference their 2350 3 Mile Rd NW Grand Rapids HQ as a direct local relationship.

**SpartanNash:** Reference Square Roots' active CEA supply to Family Fare, D&W, VG's Grocery as precedent — they have CEA-buying experience and processes in place.

**Whole Foods:** Reference Local Forager Program; emphasize organic certification timeline or non-GMO verified status; lead with specialty items (microgreens, specialty herbs).
