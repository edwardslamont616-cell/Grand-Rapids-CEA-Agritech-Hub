Dear Water Resources Division Staff,

I am writing on behalf of the **CEA Campus Design Council**, developers of the proposed Grand Rapids Controlled-Environment Agriculture (CEA) Agritech Hub at 555 Monroe Ave NE, Grand Rapids, MI 49503 (the "Project"). We are in the pre-feasibility planning stage for a prospective small hydroelectric installation on the Grand River and respectfully request a **pre-application meeting** with Water Resources Division staff to discuss Part 301 (Inland Lakes and Streams) and Part 303 (Wetlands Protection) requirements and the regulatory context before any application is filed.

### Project Description (Preliminary — Subject to Change)

The Project team is evaluating the technical feasibility of a small run-of-river hydroelectric facility, estimated at **100–400 kilowatts (kW)** installed capacity, located at or adjacent to the Grand River in Kent County, Michigan, in the reach generally between the Monroe Ave NE bridge and the Fulton Street corridor. We are investigating a **side-channel bypass configuration** — not an in-channel impoundment — that would divert a portion of river flow through a turbine house on the bank and return flow to the river downstream.

We are fully aware of the **Lower Grand River Habitat Restoration Project** now under construction by the City of Grand Rapids and Grand Rapids Whitewater (NRCS PL-566 funding, $11.03M; City construction contract with Taplin Group LLC, $14.56M). We intend to engage with this process cooperatively and to understand how any prospective hydro installation would need to be designed in coordination with the restored grade control structures.

The current inquiry is exploratory. No application is being filed at this time. We seek pre-application guidance on:

1. **Applicable permit requirements:** Which permits under Part 301 and/or Part 303 would apply to a side-channel bypass intake structure, turbine house, tailrace discharge, and any access infrastructure? Would a Joint Permit Application (JPA) be appropriate?

2. **Ordinary High Water Mark (OHWM) determination:** What is the OHWM at the project reach, and how would it affect the footprint of regulated activity?

3. **Wetland delineation:** Are there regulated wetlands within or adjacent to the proposed project area that would require assessment or mitigation under Part 303?

4. **ESA coordination:** Given the presence of the federally endangered snuffbox mussel (*Epioblasma triquetra*) in this reach, what state coordination with MDNR would be required, and at what stage?

5. **Section 401 Water Quality Certification:** When would a federal Section 401 CWC from EGLE be required, and would it be processed concurrently with a Part 301/303 JPA?

6. **Relationship to restoration project:** What is EGLE's position on hydroelectric installations in or adjacent to reaches subject to active habitat restoration under a NRCS-funded project? Are there permit conditions or easements arising from the Lower Reach project that would affect third-party structures in this reach?

7. **Pre-application consultation process:** Does WRD offer a formal pre-application meeting for projects of this type, and what materials (conceptual site plan, hydraulic study summary, etc.) should we prepare in advance?

### Project Context

The CEA Campus Design Council represents a multi-stakeholder development team including engineering, horticulture, educational programming, and investment professionals. The Agritech Hub is being developed as an Opportunity Zone investment. We are committed to environmental stewardship and to designing any energy infrastructure in a manner that complements — not conflicts with — the Grand River Revitalization initiative.

Our current position is that **no in-channel structures will be proposed**, and any future application will be designed around the completed grade control structures following the Lower Reach construction.
